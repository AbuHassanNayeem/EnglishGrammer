PRAGMATICS & DISCOURSE ───────────────────────────────────→ Level 7
│
├── Pragmatics
│   ├── Speech Acts (Searle 1969/1975)
│   │   ├── Assertives (commit speaker: state, claim → "The door is open")
│   │   ├── Directives (get hearer action: request, command → "Pass the salt")
│   │   ├── Commissives (commit speaker action: promise, vow → "I'll help")
│   │   ├── Expressives (express attitude: thank, apologize → "Sorry!")
│   │   └── Declarations (change reality: declare, baptize → "I pronounce you married")
│   ├── Implicature
│   │   ├── Conversational (Grice 1975: Cooperative Principle + 4 Maxims)
│   │   │   - Quantity (sufficient info), Quality (truthful), Relation (relevant), Manner (clear/brief/orderly)
│   │   │     e.g., "Some students passed" → implicates "not all"
│   │   └── Conventional (semantic, non-cancellable)
│   │       e.g., "Can you pass the salt?" → request (not literal ability Q)
│   └── Deixis (context-dependent reference; Levinson 1983)
|       |
|       ├── Person Deixis (reference to speech participants and others)
|       │   ├── First Person (speaker: I/me/my/mine/we/us/our/ours)
|       │   ├── Second Person (addressee: you/your/yours)
|       │   └── Third Person (others: he/him/his/she/her/it/its/they/them/their/theirs)
|       │
|       ├── Place (Spatial) Deixis (reference to locations relative to speaker)
|       │   ├── Proximal (near speaker: here/this/these)
|       │   └── Distal (far from speaker: there/that/those)
|       │
|       ├── Time (Temporal) Deixis (reference to time relative to the moment of utterance)
|       │   ├── Proximal (near coding time: now/today/this week)
|       │   └── Distal (distant from coding time: then/yesterday/tomorrow/last week/next month)
|       │
|       ├── Discourse Deixis (reference to portions of the discourse itself)
|       │   ├── Anaphoric (reference to preceding discourse: this/that/the above/the preceding)
|       │   └── Cataphoric (reference to following discourse: the following/the next/below)
|       │
|       └── Social Deixis (encoding of social relationships/distinctions)
|           ├── Honorifics (titles of respect: sir/ma'am/Your Honor/Doctor)
|           └── Relational (terms reflecting relationships: mate/boss/dear/honey)
|
├── Information Structure
│   ├── Topic-Comment (Chafe 1976: "Topic (given) + Comment (new)")
│   │   e.g., "The exam, John passed"
│   ├── Focus Types
│   │   ├── New Info Focus (accented: "JOHN passed")
│   │   ├── Cleft ("It was JOHN who passed")
│   │   └── Contrastive ("JOHN passed, not Mary")
│   └── Given-New Ordering (Prince 1981: Given (rheme) → New (theme) for flow)
│
└── Discourse & Cohesion (Halliday & Hasan 1976)
    ├── Cohesion (surface ties)
    │   ├── Reference (anaphora: John...he; cataphora: he...John)
    │   ├── Substitution (one/do/so → book/one)
    │   ├── Ellipsis (verbal: John left; nominal: Mary __ too)
    │   └── Conjunction (additive: and; adversative: but; temporal: then/after)
    ├── Lexical Cohesion
    │   ├── Repetition (book...book), Synonyms (happy...joyful), Hyponyms (flower...rose)
    │   └── Collocation (fish...nets...bait)
    └── Coherence (deeper logic: not just ties, but genre/text-type conventions)
        ├── Logical Flow (cause-effect, problem-solution)
        ├── Schema/Script (e.g., restaurant script: order→eat→pay)
        └── Genre (narrative, argumentative, instructional)
