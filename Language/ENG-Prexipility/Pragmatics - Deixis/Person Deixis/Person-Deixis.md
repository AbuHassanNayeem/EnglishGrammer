GRAMMAR TOPIC: Person Deixis
│
├── Definition: Person deixis identifies discourse participants via pronouns (speaker/addressee/3rd party); encodes subjective perspective shifts (Levinson 1983).
│
├── Functions/Purpose:
│ ├── Fills *subject/object roles* (pro-drop replacement)
│ ├── Signals *participant tracking* (who speaks/to whom/about whom)
│ └── Maintains *discourse roles* (I-perspective dominance)
│
├── Structure/Types:
│ ├── Speaker (1st person)
│ │ ├── Pattern: *I/me/my/mine* → subjective/possessive/object
│ │ ├── Examples:
│ │ │ ├── *I* am leaving.
│ │ │ ├── Give it to *me*.
│ │ │ └── This is *mine*.
│ │ └── Exam Tip: *I* always capitalized (IELTS basics)
│ ├── Addressee (2nd person)
│ │ ├── Pattern: *you/your/yours* → universal subject/object
│ │ ├── Examples:
│ │ │ ├── *You* should go.
│ │ │ ├── Is this *yours*?
│ │ │ └── Tell *you* later.
│ │ └── Exam Tip: Singular/plural *you* identical (TOEFL ambiguity)
│ ├── 3rd Party (non-participant)
│ │ ├── Pattern: *he/she/it/they/them* → gender/number marked
│ │ ├── Examples:
│ │ │ ├── *He* won the race.
│ │ │ ├── Call *them*.
│ │ │ └── *It* is raining.
│ │ └── Exam Tip: *They/them* = singular gender-neutral (GRE 2023)
│ └── Advanced: Inclusive/Exclusive *we*
│     ├── *We* = *you + I* (inclusive: Let's go *we*)
│     └── *We* = *I + others* (exclusive: *We* went without you)
│
├── Key Patterns & Word Order:
│ ├── Subject-Verb: *I/You/He* + V. → *I* run, *you* run, *he* runs.
│ └── Possessive: *My/your/his* + N. → *My* book, *your* pen.
│
├── Common Exam Traps (Memorize these!):
│ ├── ❌ "*me* am going" → ✅ "*I* am going" (subject form)
│ ├── ❌ "*He* don't know" → ✅ "*He* doesn't know" (3rd singular -s)
│ └── ❌ "Give *I* the book" → ✅ "Give *me* the book" (object case)
│
└── Quick Revision Mnemonics:
    ├── **1**=*I* (speaker), **2**=*You* (hearer), **3**=*He/She/It* (others)
    └── Person deixis = "*I* speak, *you* hear, *they* act."
