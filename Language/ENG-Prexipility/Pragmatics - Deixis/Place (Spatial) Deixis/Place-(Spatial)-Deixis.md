GRAMMAR TOPIC: Spatial Deixis
│
├── Definition: Spatial deixis uses location expressions relative to speaker's position (*here* vs *there*); encodes proximal/distal relations in physical space (Levinson 1983).
│
├── Functions/Purpose:
│ ├── Specifies *locative adverbials* (AdvP in VP)
│ ├── Signals *spatial perspective* (speaker-centered)
│ └── Guides *discourse navigation* (scene setting/direction)
│
├── Structure/Types:
│ ├── Proximal (speaker-near)
│ │ ├── Pattern: *Adv/Det → here/this [location]*
│ │ ├── Examples:
│ │ │ ├── Put it *here*.
│ │ │ ├── *This way* to the exit.
│ │ │ └── Sit *here* please.
│ │ └── Exam Tip: Present tense with *here* (IELTS spatial present)
│ ├── Distal (speaker-far)
│ │ ├── Pattern: *Adv/Det → there/that [location]*
│ │ ├── Examples:
│ │ │ ├── The keys are *there*.
│ │ │ ├── Go *that way*.
│ │ │ └── Look *there* on the shelf.
│ │ └── Exam Tip: No tense change needed (TOEFL verb trap)
│ └── Advanced: Extended spatial
│     ├── *This room/house* (proximal NP)
│     ├── *That building/city* (distal NP)
│     └── *Over there* (emphatic distal)
│
├── Key Patterns & Word Order:
│ ├── Locative AdvP: V + *here/there*. → Stand *here*. / Go *there*.
│ └── Directional: *This/That* way + PP. → Take *this way* through the park.
│
├── Common Exam Traps (Memorize these!):
│ ├── ❌ "Put it *there* on table" → ✅ "Put it *here* on the table" (proximal logic)
│ ├── ❌ "*Here* is the keys" → ✅ "The keys are *here*" (copula required)
│ └── ❌ "Go *this way* it is faster" → ✅ "Go *this way*; it is faster." (punctuation)
│
└── Quick Revision Mnemonics:
    ├── **P**roximal = **P**oint **H**ere (*here/this*), **D**istal = **D**istant **T**here
    └── Space deixis = "*Here* = home base, *there* = away game."
