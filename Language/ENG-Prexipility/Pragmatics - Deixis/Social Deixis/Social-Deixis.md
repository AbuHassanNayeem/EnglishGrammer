GRAMMAR TOPIC: Social Deixis
│
├── Definition: Social deixis encodes speaker-hearer social relationship, status, or familiarity via address forms; 
    relies on cultural/contextual norms beyond person deixis (Levinson 1983).
│
├── Functions/Purpose:
│ ├── Encodes *politeness/power* (T/V distinction: formal tu/usted)
│ ├── Signals *solidarity/distance* (in-group vs. out-group)
│ └── Manages *discourse rapport* (builds/maintains social bonds)
│
├── Structure/Types:
│ ├── Relational (familiarity-based)
│ │ ├── Pattern: *NP → Vocative N* (pre/post utterance)
│ │ ├── Examples:
│ │ │ ├── *Mate*, pass the salt! (Aussie informal)
│ │ │ ├── Hey *boss*, great job today.
│ │ │ └── *Honey*, dinner's ready. (intimate)
│ │ └── Exam Tip: *Over-formalize* in casual contexts fails IELTS rapport
│ ├── Honorifics (status-based)
│ │ ├── Pattern: *Title + NP* or *Possessive + Title*
│ │ ├── Examples:
│ │ │ ├── *Sir*, your table is ready. (service)
│ │ │ ├── *Your Honor*, the defense rests.
│ │ │ └── *Ma'am*, may I help you? (respect)
│ │ └── Exam Tip: **Capitalize** titles in writing (GRE trap)
│ └── Advanced: Hybrid (culture-specific)
│     ├── *Ji* (Hindi respect: *Guru-ji*)
│     ├── *San* (Japanese: *Tanaka-san*)
│     └── *Sahib* (South Asia: *Doctor Sahib*)
│
├── Key Patterns & Word Order:
│ ├── Vocative comma: "*Name*, [statement]." → *John*, close the door.
│ └── Title precedence: "**Dr.** Smith" > "Dr. *Smith*" (formal first)
│
├── Common Exam Traps (Memorize these!):
│ ├── ❌ "hey Sir, sit down" → ✅ "*Sir*, please sit down" (politeness)
│ ├── ❌ "boss you late" → ✅ "*Boss*, you're late" (comma/vocative order)
│ └── ❌ "maam" → ✅ "**Ma'am**" (capitalization + apostrophe)
│
└── Quick Revision Mnemonics:
    ├── **R**elational = **R**elaxed (*mate/honey*), **H**onorific = **H**ierarchy (*sir/ma'am*)
    └── Social deixis = "*Status* shows in *salutation*."
