GRAMMAR TOPIC: Temporal Deixis
│
├── Definition: Temporal deixis uses time expressions relative to speaking moment (*now* vs *then*); encodes proximity/distal relations in time (Levinson 1983).
│
├── Functions/Purpose:
│ ├── Anchors *tense/aspect* (present/past/future reference)
│ ├── Signals *temporal flow* (proximate vs remote events)
│ └── Creates *discourse timeline* (narrative sequencing)
│
├── Structure/Types:
│ ├── Proximal (near speech time)
│ │ ├── Pattern: *Adv/NP → now/today/this [time unit]*
│ │ ├── Examples:
│ │ │ ├── I'm leaving *now*.
│ │ │ ├── Let's meet *today*.
│ │ │ └── This week is busy.
│ │ └── Exam Tip: Use present tense with proximal (IELTS verb agreement)
│ ├── Distal (remote from speech time)
│ │ ├── Pattern: *Adv/NP → then/yesterday/tomorrow + past/future*
│ │ ├── Examples:
│ │ │ ├── He arrived *then*.
│ │ │ ├── I saw her *yesterday*.
│ │ │ └── We'll go *tomorrow*.
│ │ └── Exam Tip: Past tense with yesterday/*then* (TOEFL trap)
│ └── Advanced: Extended periods
│     ├── *This month/year* (proximal: future planning)
│     ├── *That day* (distal: specific past)
│     └── *Next week* (proximal future)
│
├── Key Patterns & Word Order:
│ ├── Proximal + present: "*Now/Today*, [present verb]." → *Now*, I understand.
│ └── Distal + tense shift: "*Yesterday/Then*, [past verb]." → *Yesterday*, it rained.
│
├── Common Exam Traps (Memorize these!):
│ ├── ❌ "*Now* I *went* home" → ✅ "*Now* I *go* home" (tense agreement)
│ ├── ❌ "*Tomorrow* it *rains*" → ✅ "*Tomorrow* it *will rain*" (future form)
│ └── ❌ "This week I *will finish*" → ✅ "*This week* I *finish*" (habitual present)
│
└── Quick Revision Mnemonics:
    ├── **P**roximal = **P**resent (*now* = *am*), **D**istal = **D**ifferent tense (*then* = *was*)
    └── Time deixis = "*Now* near, *then* far."
