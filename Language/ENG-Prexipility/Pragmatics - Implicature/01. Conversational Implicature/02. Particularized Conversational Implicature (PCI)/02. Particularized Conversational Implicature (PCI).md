Particularized Conversational Implicature (PCI)
├── **Definition**
│   ├── A type of conversational implicature that arises **only in specific contexts**.
│   ├── Requires **situational, cultural, or discourse information** to be inferred.
│   └── Contrasts with Generalized Conversational Implicature (GCI), which is largely context-independent.
│
├── **Core Function**
│   ├── To convey indirect meaning that depends on the **listener’s ability to infer context**.
│   ├── To allow nuanced, context-sensitive communication.
│   ├── Often used for politeness, irony, humor, or subtle insinuation.
│   └── Tested in competitive exams for **context-based interpretation skills**.
│
├── **Main Types / Categories**
│   ├── **Contextual Implicature**
│   │   ├── **Definition**: Meaning derived from **immediate situational context**.
│   │   ├── **Function**: Allows listener to infer unstated intention or request.
│   │   ├── **Examples**
│   │   │   ├── *Can you pass the salt?* → request, not literal question.  
│   │   │   └── *It’s cold in here.* → may imply *Please close the window*.  
│   │   ├── **Variations / Forms**
│   │   │   ├── Direct vs indirect request  
│   │   │   └── Politeness mitigated forms  
│   │   ├── **Transformations**
│   │   │   └── Explicit phrasing: *Please pass the salt.*  
│   │   └── **Exam Tip**
│   │       └── “Always check situational cues; PCI relies on context, unlike GCI.”
│   │
│   ├── **Irony / Sarcasm Implicature**
│   │   ├── **Definition**: Meaning inferred through **contrast between literal statement and situation**.
│   │   ├── **Function**: Conveys criticism, humor, or emphasis indirectly.
│   │   ├── **Examples**
│   │   │   ├── *Great weather!* → said during a storm → implies *bad weather*.  
│   │   │   └── *What a brilliant move!* → said sarcastically → implies *poor decision*.  
│   │   ├── **Variations / Forms**
│   │   │   ├── Sarcasm: direct contrast  
│   │   │   └── Irony: situational mismatch  
│   │   ├── **Transformations**
│   │   │   └── Can be paraphrased with explicit contrast: *He made a poor decision, ironically called brilliant.*  
│   │   └── **Exam Tip**
│   │       └── “MCQs often test tone recognition; irony is always context-dependent.”
│   │
│   ├── **Politeness / Face-Saving Implicature**
│   │   ├── **Definition**: Implicature used to **mitigate potential face-threats** in conversation.
│   │   ├── **Function**: Softens requests, criticisms, or negative statements.
│   │   ├── **Examples**
│   │   │   ├── *I was wondering if you could help me…* → indirect request.  
│   │   │   └── *It’s a bit noisy here.* → subtle complaint.  
│   │   ├── **Variations / Forms**
│   │   │   ├── Formal vs informal registers  
│   │   │   └── Hedging expressions: could, might, perhaps  
│   │   ├── **Transformations**
│   │   │   └── Explicit: *Please help me.*  
│   │   └── **Exam Tip**
│   │       └── “Check for politeness markers and hedges; PCI depends on interpersonal context.”
│   │
│   ├── **Irony + Politeness Interaction**
│   │   ├── **Definition**: PCI may combine **irony and politeness** simultaneously.
│   │   ├── **Function**: Delivers subtle, indirect meaning while maintaining social norms.
│   │   ├── **Examples**
│   │   │   ├── *I’m sure you worked very hard!* → may be sarcastic yet polite.  
│   │   │   └── *You did your best, unfortunately…* → soft criticism.  
│   │   └── **Exam Tip**
│   │       └── “Always analyze tone + context together; frequent GRE/IELTS MCQ trap.”
│   │
│   └── **Humor / Rhetorical Implicature**
│       ├── **Definition**: PCI that **depends on context for humor or rhetorical effect**.
│       ├── **Function**: Engages listener; relies on inference, exaggeration, or understatement.
│       ├── **Examples**
│       │   ├── *I just love waiting in traffic!* → humorous exaggeration.  
│       │   └── *He’s a genius, of course!* → may imply doubt depending on context.  
│       └── **Exam Tip**
│           └── “MCQs may present literal vs intended meaning; context rules.”
│
├── **Advanced / Edge Cases**
│   ├── Interaction with GCI
│   │   └── PCI arises **only in special context**, whereas GCI occurs by default.
│   ├── Cancellability
│   │   └── Almost always cancellable: *“He may be tired, but not necessarily.”*
│   ├── Cultural / Register Dependence
│   │   └── PCI meaning can vary by cultural norms or formal/informal settings.
│   ├── Embedded / Nested PCI
│   │   └── Occurs in subordinate clauses or multi-clause discourse.
│   └── Interaction with Presupposition and Entailment
│       └── PCI is inference-based, not presupposed or entailed; easily overridden.
│
├── **Common Mistakes**
│   ├── Confusing PCI with GCI → context check is essential.  
│   ├── Missing irony or politeness cues.  
│   ├── Over-attributing meaning where literal suffices.  
│   ├── Ignoring register / culture → wrong MCQ answer.  
│   └── Failing to recognize cancellability in MCQs.
│
├── **Comparison with Similar Structures**
│   ├── **Generalized Conversational Implicature (GCI)**
│   │   ├── Default, context-independent  
│   │   └── PCI is context-sensitive  
│   ├── **Presupposition**
│   │   ├── Background assumption, survives negation  
│   │   └── PCI can be cancelled  
│   └── **Entailment**
│       ├── Truth-preserving, non-cancellable  
│       └── PCI is inference-based, cancellable depending on context
│
└── **Quick Revision Rules**
    ├── “PCI = context-dependent; GCI = default.”  
    ├── “Always check situational cues, tone, and speaker intention.”  
    ├── “Irony, sarcasm, politeness, and humor → classic PCI triggers.”  
    ├── “Cancellable? Usually yes; look for explicit negation.”  
    ├── “Contrast with literal meaning carefully in MCQs.”  
    └── “Cultural and register awareness is key for TOEFL/IELTS/SSC exams.”
