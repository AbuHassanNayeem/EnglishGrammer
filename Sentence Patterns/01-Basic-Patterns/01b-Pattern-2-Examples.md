# Pattern 2: S + V + O — Detailed Examples

## Pattern Structure

**S + V + O** (Subject + Transitive Verb + Direct Object)

This pattern uses a **transitive verb** that requires a direct object to complete its meaning. The object receives the action and answers "what?" or "whom?" after the verb.

---

## Common Transitive Verbs by Category

### Communication Verbs
- ask, answer, explain, mention, say, tell, discuss, describe, announce, declare

### Cognitive Verbs
- know, understand, believe, think, remember, forget, recognize, realize, consider

### Physical Action Verbs
- buy, sell, make, break, build, destroy, open, close, push, pull, throw, catch

### Sensory Verbs
- see, hear, smell, taste, touch, feel, watch, observe, notice

### Possession/Transfer Verbs
- have, own, possess, get, receive, obtain, lose, find, acquire, gain

### Creation Verbs
- write, draw, paint, create, compose, design, produce, develop

## Different Object Types

### 1. Noun Objects
- She reads **books**. (O: books — noun)
- He bought **a car**. (O: a car — noun phrase)
- They love **chocolate**. (O: chocolate — noun)

### 2. Pronoun Objects
- I saw **him**. (O: him — pronoun)
- She met **them**. (O: them — pronoun)
- He knows **me**. (O: me — pronoun)
- We trust **her**. (O: her — pronoun)
- They called **us**. (O: us — pronoun)

### 3. Noun Clause Objects
- I know **that she is honest**. (O: that she is honest — noun clause)
- She believes **what he said**. (O: what he said — wh-clause)
- He understands **why it happened**. (O: why it happened — wh-clause)
- They realize **that time is limited**. (O: that time is limited — noun clause)
- We discovered **where she lives**. (O: where she lives — wh-clause)

### 4. Gerund Objects (Verbal Noun)
- He enjoys **swimming**. (O: swimming — gerund)
- She loves **dancing**. (O: dancing — gerund)
- I hate **waiting**. (O: waiting — gerund)
- They started **running**. (O: running — gerund)
- We finished **eating**. (O: eating — gerund)

### 5. Infinitive Objects
- She wants **to leave**. (O: to leave — infinitive)
- He plans **to study abroad**. (O: to study abroad — infinitive phrase)
- I hope **to succeed**. (O: to succeed — infinitive)
- They decided **to go**. (O: to go — infinitive)
- We expected **to win**. (O: to win — infinitive)

## Passive Voice Transformation

Pattern 2 sentences **can be made passive** by promoting the object to subject position:

### Active → Passive Examples

1. **Active:** She reads books.
   - **Passive:** Books are read by her.

2. **Active:** He bought a car.
   - **Passive:** A car was bought by him.

3. **Active:** They built the house.
   - **Passive:** The house was built by them.

4. **Active:** I wrote the letter.
   - **Passive:** The letter was written by me.

5. **Active:** She painted the picture.
   - **Passive:** The picture was painted by her.

6. **Active:** We watched the movie.
   - **Passive:** The movie was watched by us.

7. **Active:** He discovered America.
   - **Passive:** America was discovered by him.

8. **Active:** They opened the store.
   - **Passive:** The store was opened by them.

9. **Active:** She solved the problem.
   - **Passive:** The problem was solved by her.

10. **Active:** I fixed the computer.
    - **Passive:** The computer was fixed by me.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ❌ Using Intransitive Verbs with Objects
- **Wrong:** She arrived the station.
- **Correct:** She arrived at the station. (Pattern 1 + adverbial, not Pattern 2)

### ❌ Pronoun Case Errors
- **Wrong:** I saw he.
- **Correct:** I saw him. (object pronoun, not subject pronoun)

### ❌ Missing Required Object
- **Wrong:** She explained. (incomplete)
- **Correct:** She explained the rule. (O: the rule)

### ❌ Confusing Object with Complement
- **Wrong pattern identification:** "She is a teacher" is NOT Pattern 2!
- **Correct:** "She is a teacher" is Pattern 3 (S + V + C) — complement, not object

---

## Pattern 2 Test: Is it an Object?

### Test 1: Ask "What?" or "Whom?"
- She reads **_____**. → What does she read? → **books** = object ✓

### Test 2: Passive Transformation
- Can you make it passive? → **Books are read by her.** ✓
- If yes, it's an object!

### Test 3: Different from Subject
- Object receives action, subject performs it
- **She** reads **books** — she ≠ books ✓

## Summary

**Pattern 2 (S + V + O)** is fundamental to English:

✅ Uses transitive verbs (requires object)  
✅ Object receives the action  
✅ **Can** form passive voice  
✅ Object answers "what?" or "whom?"  
✅ Object different from subject  
✅ Most common sentence pattern in English

**Key Test:** Does the verb need something/someone to complete its meaning? If yes, and it's not describing the subject, it's Pattern 2!
