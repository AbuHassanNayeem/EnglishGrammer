# Pattern 4: S + V + A — Detailed Examples

## Pattern Structure

**S + V + A** (Subject + Verb + Adverbial)

This pattern includes an **adverbial** that provides essential information. Some verbs require an adverbial to complete their meaning (e.g., *live, reside, put, place*). The adverbial answers questions like where, when, how, or how often.

---

## Types of Adverbials

### Place Adverbials (Where?)
Essential for verbs like: live, reside, stay, dwell, work, study

### Time Adverbials (When?)
Essential for verbs like: occur, happen, take place, start, begin, end

### Manner Adverbials (How?)
Essential for describing how an action is performed

### Frequency Adverbials (How often?)
Describing regularity of actions

## Pattern 4 vs Pattern 1 (Key Difference)

### Pattern 4: Adverbial REQUIRED

Some verbs need an adverbial to complete meaning:

- **Incomplete:** He lives. ✗ (where?)
- **Complete:** He lives in Dhaka. ✓ (Pattern 4)

- **Incomplete:** She put the book. ✗ (where? — this is actually Pattern 7)
- **Complete:** She put the book on the table. ✓ (Pattern 7: has object too)

### Pattern 1: Adverbial OPTIONAL

Complete without adverbial:

- **Complete:** He sleeps. ✓ (Pattern 1)
- **Also fine:** He sleeps early. ✓ (Pattern 1 + optional adverbial)

### Test: Can you remove the adverbial?

**Pattern 4:**
- She lives in Dhaka. → She lives. ✗ (incomplete)
- Therefore: adverbial is REQUIRED (Pattern 4)

**Pattern 1 + Optional A:**
- He sleeps early. → He sleeps. ✓ (still complete)
- Therefore: adverbial is OPTIONAL (Pattern 1)

## Verbs That Typically Require Adverbials

### Place Adverbials Required:
- live, reside, dwell, stay (where?)
- work, study (where?)
- sit, stand, lie (where?)
- wait (where?)

### Time Adverbials Common:
- arrive, depart, leave (when?)
- start, begin, end, finish (when?)
- happen, occur, take place (when?)

### Manner Adverbials Common:
- behave (how?)
- act, function (how?)
- perform (how?)

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ❌ Omitting Required Adverbial
- **Wrong:** She lives. (where?)
- **Correct:** She lives in Tokyo. (A: in Tokyo)

### ❌ Confusing Pattern 4 with Pattern 2
- **Pattern 4:** She lives in Dhaka. (A: in Dhaka — not an object)
- **Pattern 2:** She loves Dhaka. (O: Dhaka — object)

Test: Can you make it passive?
- **Pattern 4:** Dhaka is lived in by her. ✗ (awkward/wrong)
- **Pattern 2:** Dhaka is loved by her. ✓

### ❌ Using Adjectives Instead of Adverbs
- **Wrong:** She drives careful. (adjective)
- **Correct:** She drives carefully. (adverb)

## Summary

**Pattern 4 (S + V + A)** uses essential adverbials:

✅ Some verbs require adverbials to complete meaning  
✅ Adverbial answers: where, when, how, how often  
✅ Forms: single word, phrase, or clause  
✅ Common with: live, work, study, arrive, happen  
✅ Test: Can the sentence stand without the adverbial?

**Key Test:** If removing the adverbial makes the sentence feel incomplete or unclear, it's Pattern 4!
