# Pattern 5: S + V + IO + DO — Detailed Examples

## Pattern Structure

**S + V + IO + DO** (Subject + Ditransitive Verb + Indirect Object + Direct Object)

This pattern uses a **ditransitive verb** that takes **two objects**: an indirect object (IO) — the recipient or beneficiary, and a direct object (DO) — the thing given, told, or done.

**Structure:** Someone gives **something** (DO) to **someone** (IO)

---

## Common Ditransitive Verbs

### Transfer Verbs (Give/Send Pattern)
- give, send, lend, hand, pass, offer, award, grant, show

### Communication Verbs (Tell Pattern)
- tell, teach, ask, write, read, email, text, promise

### Creation/Acquisition Verbs (Make/Buy Pattern with "for")
- make, buy, get, cook, bake, build, find, order, fetch

### Other Ditransitive Verbs
- bring, take, wish, owe, pay, sell, throw, toss, cost

## Alternative Pattern: S + V + DO + to/for + IO

Many Pattern 5 sentences can be restructured using "to" or "for":

### Using "TO" (Transfer/Communication)

**Standard Pattern 5:**
1. She gave me a pen. → She gave a pen **to me**.
2. He sent her a letter. → He sent a letter **to her**.
3. They told us the truth. → They told the truth **to us**.
4. I showed him the way. → I showed the way **to him**.
5. She taught me English. → She taught English **to me**.
6. He lent them money. → He lent money **to them**.
7. She handed me the keys. → She handed the keys **to me**.
8. I passed her the salt. → I passed the salt **to her**.
9. He read them a story. → He read a story **to them**.
10. She sold me her car. → She sold her car **to me**.

### Using "FOR" (Benefit/Creation)

**Standard Pattern 5:**
1. They bought him a gift. → They bought a gift **for him**.
2. She made me a cake. → She made a cake **for me**.
3. He cooked her dinner. → He cooked dinner **for her**.
4. I baked them cookies. → I baked cookies **for them**.
5. We built him a house. → We built a house **for him**.
6. She found me a job. → She found a job **for me**.
7. He got her a ticket. → He got a ticket **for her**.
8. They ordered us food. → They ordered food **for us**.

---

## When to Use "TO" vs "FOR"

### Use "TO" (Transfer/Communication)
Verbs of transfer or communication: give, send, tell, show, teach, lend, hand, pass, sell, throw, offer, write, read, bring, take

**Meaning:** Something is transferred or communicated TO someone

### Use "FOR" (Benefit/Purpose)
Verbs of creation or acquisition: make, buy, cook, bake, build, find, get, order, fetch

**Meaning:** Something is done FOR the benefit of someone

## Passive Voice Transformations

Pattern 5 can form passive in **TWO ways**:

### Option 1: IO Becomes Subject

**Active:** She gave me a pen.
- **Passive:** I was given a pen (by her).

**Active:** He told them a story.
- **Passive:** They were told a story (by him).

**Active:** She taught me English.
- **Passive:** I was taught English (by her).

### Option 2: DO Becomes Subject

**Active:** She gave me a pen.
- **Passive:** A pen was given to me (by her).

**Active:** He told them a story.
- **Passive:** A story was told to them (by him).

**Active:** She taught me English.
- **Passive:** English was taught to me (by her).

## Identification Tests

### Test 1: Ask "What?" and "To/For Whom?"

**She gave me a pen.**
- What did she give? → **a pen** (DO)
- To whom did she give it? → **me** (IO)

### Test 2: Rephrase with "to" or "for"

**He sent her flowers.**
- He sent flowers **to her**. ✓
- Therefore: **her** = IO, **flowers** = DO

### Test 3: Two Passive Forms

If you can make two different passive sentences, it's Pattern 5:
- Active: She gave me a pen.
- Passive 1: I was given a pen.
- Passive 2: A pen was given to me.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ❌ Wrong Word Order
- **Wrong:** She gave to me a pen.
- **Correct:** She gave me a pen. (IO before DO)
- **or:** She gave a pen to me. (DO + to + IO)

### ❌ Confusing IO with DO
- IO = recipient (who receives)
- DO = thing (what is given/told)

### ❌ Using Wrong Preposition
- **Wrong:** She made a cake to me. (should be "for")
- **Correct:** She made a cake for me.
- **or:** She made me a cake.

### ❌ Both Objects as Pronouns (Awkward)
- **Awkward:** She gave him it.
- **Better:** She gave it to him.

## Summary

**Pattern 5 (S + V + IO + DO)** has two objects:

✅ IO = recipient/beneficiary (to/for whom)  
✅ DO = thing given/told (what)  
✅ Standard order: IO before DO  
✅ Alternative: DO + to/for + IO  
✅ Can form TWO passive constructions  
✅ Use "to" for transfer/communication verbs  
✅ Use "for" for benefit/creation verbs

**Key Test:** Can you rephrase with "to" or "for"? If yes, and there are two objects, it's Pattern 5!
