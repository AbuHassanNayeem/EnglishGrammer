# Pattern 6: S + V + O + C — Detailed Examples

## Pattern Structure

**S + V + O + C** (Subject + Complex-Transitive Verb + Object + Object Complement)

This pattern uses a **complex-transitive verb** that takes both a direct object and an **object complement**. The object complement describes, identifies, or renames the direct object (not the subject).

**Key:** Object Complement describes the OBJECT, not the subject

---

## Common Complex-Transitive Verbs

### Verbs of Appointment/Selection
- elect, name, call, appoint, crown, declare, proclaim, designate

### Verbs of Causation (Make/Cause)
- make, cause, drive, render, leave, keep

### Verbs of Perception/Judgment
- consider, think, believe, find, judge, deem, prove

### Verbs of Transformation
- paint, color, dye, turn

### Other Complex-Transitive Verbs
- get, set, want, like, prefer

## Object Complement Types

### 1. Noun Complements

**Profession/Role:**
- They elected him **president**. (O: him = C: president)
- She made me **captain**. (O: me = C: captain)
- We appointed her **manager**. (O: her = C: manager)

**Identity:**
- They named the baby **Sarah**. (O: the baby = C: Sarah)
- We call him **the boss**. (O: him = C: the boss)

### 2. Adjective Complements

**State/Quality:**
- The movie made me **sad**. (O: me, C: sad)
- She painted the room **red**. (O: the room, C: red)
- I found the test **difficult**. (O: the test, C: difficult)
- They kept the house **clean**. (O: the house, C: clean)
- He left the door **open**. (O: the door, C: open)

### 3. Participle Complements

**Present Participle (-ing):**
- She kept me **waiting**. (O: me, C: waiting)
- He left the engine **running**. (O: the engine, C: running)
- They found him **sleeping**. (O: him, C: sleeping)
- I got the machine **working**. (O: the machine, C: working)

**Past Participle (-ed):**
- I got my car **fixed**. (O: my car, C: fixed)
- She had her hair **cut**. (O: her hair, C: cut)
- They left me **confused**. (O: me, C: confused)
- We kept the door **closed**. (O: the door, C: closed)

## Passive Voice

**Active:** They elected him president.
- **Passive:** He was elected president (by them).

**Active:** She painted the room red.
- **Passive:** The room was painted red (by her).

**Active:** I found the test difficult.
- **Passive:** The test was found difficult (by me).

**Active:** They kept the house clean.
- **Passive:** The house was kept clean (by them).

**Note:** The complement remains with the object even in passive voice!

## Pattern 6 Tests

### Test 1: Complement Describes Object (Not Subject)

**Pattern 6:**
- They elected **him** **president**. (president describes HIM, not THEY)
- She painted **the room** **red**. (red describes THE ROOM, not SHE)

### Test 2: Insert "as" or "to be"

- They elected him **(as)** president. ✓
- She painted the room **(to be)** red. ✓
- The movie made me **(to be)** sad. ✓

### Test 3: Object = Complement in Meaning

- him = president (They're the same person)
- the room = red (The room has the quality of being red)

---

## Pattern 6 vs Pattern 5 (Key Difference)

### Pattern 6: Object = Complement
- **They elected him president.** (him = president) ✓
- Complement describes the OBJECT

### Pattern 5: Two Different Things
- **They gave him a book.** (him ≠ book) ✓
- Two separate objects (recipient + thing)

### Test: Are they the same?

**Pattern 6:**
- They elected him president. → him = president ✓

**Pattern 5:**
- They gave him a book. → him = book? ✗ (different things)

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ❌ Confusing Pattern 6 with Pattern 5
- **Pattern 6:** They made him captain. (him = captain)
- **Pattern 5:** They made him breakfast. (him ≠ breakfast)

### ❌ Missing the Complement
- **Wrong:** They elected him. (incomplete — elected to what?)
- **Correct:** They elected him president. (C: president)

### ❌ Using Adverbs Instead of Adjectives
- **Wrong:** The movie made me sadly.
- **Correct:** The movie made me sad. (adjective complement)

## Summary

**Pattern 6 (S + V + O + C)** has object complement:

✅ Complement describes/identifies the OBJECT
✅ Object = Complement in meaning
✅ Can insert "as" or "to be"
✅ Complement types: noun, adjective, participle
✅ Common verbs: elect, make, consider, find, paint, keep
✅ Passive keeps complement with object

**Key Test:** Does the word after the object describe the object? If yes, it's Pattern 6!
