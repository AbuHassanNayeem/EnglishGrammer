# Pattern 7: S + V + O + A ‚Äî Detailed Examples

## Pattern Structure

**S + V + O + A** (Subject + Transitive Verb + Object + Adverbial)

This pattern includes a transitive verb with a direct object, followed by an **adverbial** that provides essential information. Some verbs require both an object AND an adverbial to complete their meaning (e.g., *put, place, set, lay*).

**Key:** Both object AND adverbial are essential

---

## Verbs Requiring Object + Adverbial

### Position/Location Verbs (ESSENTIAL Adverbial)
- put, place, set, lay, position, locate, situate

### Movement/Transfer Verbs (Common with Adverbial)
- throw, toss, push, pull, move, carry, bring, take, send

### Discovery/Finding Verbs
- find, discover, locate, spot

### Leaving Verbs
- leave, abandon, deposit

## Adverbial Types in Pattern 7

### 1. Place Adverbials (ESSENTIAL with Put/Place/Set)

**Prepositional Phrases:**
- He put the book **on the table**. (A: on the table)
- She placed the vase **on the shelf**. (A: on the shelf)
- I set the cup **down**. (A: down)

**Directional Adverbs:**
- She put her bag **down**. (A: down)
- He threw the ball **away**. (A: away)
- They moved the furniture **upstairs**. (A: upstairs)

### 2. Time Adverbials (Common but not always essential)

- They finished the work **yesterday**. (A: yesterday)
- She sent the email **this morning**. (A: this morning)
- I completed the project **on time**. (A: on time)

### 3. Manner Adverbials (Describe how the action was done)

- He explained the concept **clearly**. (A: clearly)
- She sang the song **beautifully**. (A: beautifully)
- They solved the problem **quickly**. (A: quickly)

## Pattern 7 Tests

### Test 1: Both Object AND Adverbial Required

**With "Put" (incomplete without both):**
- **Incomplete:** He put. ‚úó (put what? where?)
- **Still incomplete:** He put the book. ‚úó (where?)
- **Complete:** He put the book on the table. ‚úì (has both O and A)

### Test 2: Remove the Adverbial

If removing the adverbial makes the sentence feel incomplete, it's Pattern 7:

- She put the keys in her pocket. ‚Üí She put the keys. ‚úó (incomplete)
- Therefore: adverbial is ESSENTIAL (Pattern 7)

vs.

- She finished the work yesterday. ‚Üí She finished the work. ‚úì (still complete)
- Therefore: adverbial is OPTIONAL (Pattern 2 + optional A)

## Pattern 7 vs Pattern 2 (Key Difference)

### Pattern 7: Adverbial ESSENTIAL

- **He put the book on the table.**
  - Remove adverbial: He put the book. ‚úó (incomplete ‚Äî where?)
  - Adverbial is REQUIRED

### Pattern 2: Adverbial OPTIONAL

- **She finished the work yesterday.**
  - Remove adverbial: She finished the work. ‚úì (still complete)
  - Adverbial is OPTIONAL

### Verbs That Require Pattern 7:
- put, place, set, lay (must say WHERE)

## Pattern 7 vs Pattern 4 (Key Difference)

### Pattern 7: Has OBJECT + Adverbial

- **He put the book on the table.**
  - Has object: the book ‚úì
  - Has adverbial: on the table ‚úì
  - Pattern 7 (S + V + O + A)

### Pattern 4: Has Adverbial (NO Object)

- **He lives in Dhaka.**
  - No object
  - Has adverbial: in Dhaka ‚úì
  - Pattern 4 (S + V + A)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ‚ùå Omitting Required Adverbial with Put/Place/Set/Lay
- **Wrong:** She put the keys. (where?)
- **Correct:** She put the keys on the desk. (A: on the desk)

### ‚ùå Confusing Lay (Transitive) with Lie (Intransitive)
- **Transitive (Pattern 7):** She **laid** the baby in the crib. (has object)
- **Intransitive (Pattern 1/4):** The baby **lay** in the crib. (no object)

### ‚ùå Confusing Essential with Optional Adverbials
- **Essential (Pattern 7):** He put the book on the shelf. (can't remove "on the shelf")
- **Optional (Pattern 2):** He read the book yesterday. (can remove "yesterday")
