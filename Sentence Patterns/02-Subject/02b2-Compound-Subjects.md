## 1. Definition
A **Compound Subject** consists of two or more simple subjects that share the same verb. They are joined by coordinating conjunctions (usually *and*, *or*, *nor*).

---

## 2. Agreement Rules

### A. Joined by "AND"
Subjects joined by **and** typically take a **PLURAL** verb.
*   ***Jack and Jill*** **are** going up the hill.
*   ***The dog and the cat*** **fight** all the time.

**Exception:** If the two nouns refer to the *same person/thing* or a *single unit*.
*   ***Peanut butter and jelly*** **is** delicious. (Considered one food dish).
*   ***The founder and CEO*** **is** speaking. (One person holds two titles).

### B. Joined by "OR" / "NOR"
When joined by **or**, **nor**, **either...or**, or **neither...nor**, the verb agrees with the subject **closest to it** (Proximity Rule).
*   *Neither the players nor the **coach*** **is** happy. (Coach is singular => is).
*   *Neither the coach nor the **players*** **are** happy. (Players is plural => are).

---

## 3. Examples

| Joiner | Example | Verb Number |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **And** | **Gold and Silver** are precious. | Plural |
| **And (Unit)** | **Fish and chips** is a popular meal. | Singular (Unit) |
| **Or** | **The boy or his parents** are coming. | Plural (matches 'parents') |
| **Neither/Nor** | **Neither John nor I** am responsible. | Singular (matches 'I') |

## 4. Practice
Choose the correct verb:
1.  Bread and butter (is/are) my daily breakfast.
2.  The teacher and the students (is/are) in the class.
3.  Neither the dog nor the cats (make/makes) noise.

> **Answers:** 1. is (Unit), 2. are, 3. make (Matches 'cats').
