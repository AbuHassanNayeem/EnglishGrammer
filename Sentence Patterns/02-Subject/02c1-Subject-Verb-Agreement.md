# Subject-Verb Agreement — Detailed Guide

## 1. The Golden Rule
The subject and verb must agree in **Number**.
*   **Singular Subject** -> **Singular Verb** (*He walks*)
*   **Plural Subject** -> **Plural Verb** (*They walk*)

---

## 2. Common Challenges

### A. Phrases Between Subject and Verb
Do not let prepositional phrases (starting with *of, in, with, along with*) trick you.
*   ❌ *The **list** of names **are** lost.*
*   ✅ *The **list** [Head] of names **is** lost.*

### B. Compound Subjects (and, or, nor)
1.  **Joined by "and":** Usually Plural. (*Jack and Jill **are**...*)
2.  **Joined by "or/nor":** Verb agrees with the **closest** subject.
    *   *Neither the teacher nor the **students are** happy.*
    *   *Neither the students nor the **teacher is** happy.*

### C. Indefinite Pronouns
*   **Always Singular:** *Each, Every, Someone, Everyone, Nobody.*
    *   ***Everyone is** here.*
*   **Variable:** *Some, Most, All.*
    *   *Most of the **cake is**...*
    *   *Most of the **cookies are**...*

### D. Collective Nouns
Nouns that name a group (Team, Family, Audience).
*   **As a unit:** Singular (*The team **is** winning.*)
*   **As individuals:** Plural (*The team **are** fighting among themselves.*)

---

## 3. Special Cases

### A. Existential "There"
The verb agrees with the noun that follows.
*   *There **is** a **problem**.*
*   *There **are** many **problems**.*

### B. Verbal/Clausal Subjects
Always Singular.
*   ***Reading books is** fun.*
*   ***That he failed was** sad.*

---

## 4. Practice Examples
1.  One of the boxes (is/are) open. -> **is** (Head: One)
2.  The players and the coach (is/are) tired. -> **are** (Joined by 'and')
3.  Either the pie or the cookies (is/are) fine. -> **are** (Closest: cookies)
