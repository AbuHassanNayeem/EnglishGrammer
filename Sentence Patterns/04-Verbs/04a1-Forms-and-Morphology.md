## 1. The Five Main Forms
Every English verb (except Modals) has distinct forms used to construct tenses.

| Form | Technical Name | Example (Regular) | Example (Irregular) | Usage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **V1** | **Base Form** (Infinitive) | walk | eat, be | Present Simple (I walk), Infinitive (To walk) |
| **V-s** | **3rd Person Singular** | walks | eats, is | Present Simple (She walks) |
| **V2** | **Past Simple** | walked | ate, was/were | Past Simple (I walked) |
| **V3** | **Past Participle** | walked | eaten, been | Perfect Tenses (Have eaten), Passive (Is eaten) |
| **V-ing** | **Present Participle** | walking | eating, being | Continuous (Is walking), Gerunds (Walking is fun) |

## 2. Regular vs. Irregular Verbs

### A. Regular Verbs
Follow a standard pattern: **add -ed** for both V2 and V3.
*   *Walk -> Walked -> Walked*
*   *Play -> Played -> Played*
*   **Spelling Rules:**
    *   Ends in 'e': add 'd' (*Dance -> Danced*)
    *   Ends in CVC (Consonant-Vowel-Consonant): double the last letter (*Stop -> Stopped*)
    *   Ends in 'y': change to 'ied' (*Study -> Studied*)

### B. Irregular Verbs
Do **not** follow the -ed rule. They must be memorized.
*   **Three forms same:** *Cut - Cut - Cut*
*   **V2/V3 same:** *Buy - Bought - Bought*
*   **All different:** *Go - Went - Gone*

## 3. Finite vs. Non-Finite Verbs

### Finite Verbs (Tensed)
*   Limit the sentence in time.
*   Agree with a subject.
*   *Example:* She **works** hard. (Tense: Present, Subject: She).

### Non-Finite Verbs (Untensed)
*   Do not show time.
*   Do not change for subject.
*   **Infinitive:** He wants **to work**.
*   **Gerund:** **Working** is hard.
*   **Participle:** The **working** man.

## 4. Practice Exercises

### Identify the Form (V1, V2, V3, V-ing, V-s)
1. She **has** gone.
2. She has **gone**.
3. He **runs** fast.
4. They are **swimming**.
5. I **saw** him.

> **Answers:**
> 1. **V-s** (3rd person singular of Have)
> 2. **V3** (Past Participle of Go)
> 3. **V-s** (3rd person singular of Run)
> 4. **V-ing** (Present Participle of Swim)
> 5. **V2** (Past Simple of See)
