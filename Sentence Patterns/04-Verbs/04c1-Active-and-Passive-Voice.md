# Active & Passive Voice — Detailed Guide

## 1. What is Voice?
Voice describes the relationship between the **Subject** and the **Verb** (Action).

*   **Active:** Subject **Does**. (*The cat chased the mouse.*)
*   **Passive:** Subject **Receives**. (*The mouse was chased by the cat.*)

---

## 2. Forming the Passive
**Formula:** **Be (Tensed) + V3 (Past Participle)**

| Tense | Active | Passive Formula | Passive Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Simple Present** | writes | am/is/are + written | *It is written.* |
| **Simple Past** | wrote | was/were + written | *It was written.* |
| **Present Cont.** | is writing | am/is/are + being + written | *It is being written.* |
| **Past Cont.** | was writing | was/were + being + written | *It was being written.* |
| **Present Perfect** | has written | has/have + been + written | *It has been written.* |
| **Future Simple** | will write | will + be + written | *It will be written.* |
| **Modal** | can write | can + be + written | *It can be written.* |

---

## 3. When to Use Passive?
1.  **Unknown Doer:** *My bike was stolen.* (I don't know who).
2.  **Unimportant Doer:** *The bridge was built in 1990.* (Builders don't matter).
3.  **Formal/Scientific:** *The mixture was heated.*
4.  **Emphasis on Object:** *A cure was finally found.*

---

## 4. Constraints
*   Only **Transitive Verbs** (verbs with objects) can be passive.
*   **Intransitive Verbs** (die, arrive, happen) **NEVER** take passive.
    *   *Correct:* The accident **happened**.
    *   *Wrong:* The accident **was happened**. ❌

---

## 5. Practice Exercises

### Convert to Passive
1. Someone cleans the office every day.
2. They cancelled the match.
3. She is singing a song.

> **Answers:**
> 1. The office **is cleaned** every day.
> 2. The match **was cancelled**.
> 3. A song **is being sung** by her.
