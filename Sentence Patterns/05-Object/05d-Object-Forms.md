## 1. Noun Phrase (Most Common)
*   *I saw **a dog**.*
*   *She bought **the big red car**.*
*   *He helped **the man in the corner**.*

## 2. Pronoun
Must be in **Objective Case**.
*   *I saw **him**.*
*   *She helped **us**.*
*   *They like **it**.*

## 3. The "It" Pronoun (Special Uses)

### A. Dummy Object
*   *I found **it** difficult to explain.*
    *   Here, "it" stands in for the real object (to explain).

## 4. Verbal Objects

### A. Gerund (V-ing)
Used after verbs like *enjoy, avoid, finish, suggest*.
*   *I enjoy **swimming**.*
*   *She finished **working**.*

### B. Infinitive (To V)
Used after verbs like *want, hope, decide, plan*.
*   *I want **to go**.*
*   *She decided **to stay**.*

### C. Gerund vs Infinitive
Some verbs change meaning!
*   *Stop **smoking**.* (Quit the habit).
*   *Stop **to smoke**.* (Pause action in order to smoke).

## 5. Clausal Objects (Noun Clauses)

### A. That-Clause
*   *I know [**that he is lying**].*
*   *She said [**(that) she was tired**].*

### B. Wh-Clause
*   *I wonder [**who did it**].*
*   *Do you know [**where he lives**]?*

### C. If/Whether Clause
Used for Yes/No questions embedded as objects.
*   *I don't know [**if he is coming**].*
*   *Ask [**whether she wants tea**].*

## 6. Practice Exercises

### Identify the Object Form:
1. I admit that I was wrong.
2. She loves dancing.
3. He wants to fly.
4. I saw her.

> **Answers:**
> 1. **Noun Clause** (That...)
> 2. **Gerund**
> 3. **Infinitive**
> 4. **Pronoun**
