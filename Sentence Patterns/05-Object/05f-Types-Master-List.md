## 1. Master Table

| Type | Definition | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. Direct Object (DO)** | Directly receives the action. Answers "What?" or "Whom?". | *She reads **books**.* |
| **2. Indirect Object (IO)** | Receiver of the DO. To/For whom. | *She gave **me** a pen.* |
| **3. Compound Object** | Two or more objects joined by conjunctions. | *He bought **pen and paper**.* |
| **4. Clausal Object** | A full dependent clause acting as object. | *I know **that she is right**.* |
| **5. Reflexive Object** | Object is the same as the Subject (-self). | *He hurt **himself**.* |
| **6. Prepositional Object** | Follows a preposition. | *He is fond of **chocolate**.* |

## 2. Detailed Notes

### A. Compound Objects
Just like compound subjects, you can have multiple items receiving the action.
*   *I saw **John and Mary**.* (Compound DO).

### B. Reflexive Objects
Used when the action reflects back on the doer.
*   *Pronouns:* Myself, yourself, himself, herself, itself, ourselves, yourselves, themselves.
*   *Note:* You cannot use a subject pronoun here. (*He hurt him* means he hurt a different male).

### C. Clausal Objects
Usually Noun Clauses starting with *that, if, whether* or *Wh-words*.
*   *I wonder **if he will come**.*
