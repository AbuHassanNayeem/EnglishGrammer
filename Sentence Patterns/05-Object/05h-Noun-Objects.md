## 1. Definition
A **Noun Object** is a noun (or noun phrase) that functions as an object (Direct, Indirect, or Prepositional). It is the most common form of object.

*   **Structure:** (Modifiers) + **Head Noun** + (Modifiers)
*   **Case:** Nouns do not change form for the object case (unlike pronouns).

---

## 2. Examples

### A. Simple Noun
*   *I caught a **fish**.*
*   *She likes **coffee**.*

### B. Modified Noun Phrase
*   *He bought **the red car**.*
*   *I saw **a man with a hat**.*

### C. Proper Noun (Names)
*   *I met **John**.*
*   *He visited **Paris**.*

---

## 3. Position
*   **Direct Object:** Usually follows the verb.
    *   *Kick **the ball**.*
*   **Indirect Object:** Comes between Verb and Direct Object.
    *   *Give **John** the ball.*
*   **Object of Preposition:** Follows the preposition.
    *   *Give it to **Mary**.*
