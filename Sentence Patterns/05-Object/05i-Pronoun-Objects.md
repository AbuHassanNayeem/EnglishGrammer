## 1. Definition
A **Pronoun Object** is a pronoun taking the place of a noun in the object position. Unlike nouns, pronouns **change form** (Case) when they become objects.

---

## 2. Forms (The Objective Case)

| Subject (Doer) | **Object (Receiver)** | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| I | **Me** | *He sees **me**.* |
| You | **You** | *I see **you**.* |
| He | **Him** | *I like **him**.* |
| She | **Her** | *I help **her**.* |
| It | **It** | *He wants **it**.* |
| We | **Us** | *They joined **us**.* |
| They | **Them** | *We found **them**.* |
| Who | **Whom** | *To **whom** did you speak?* |

---

## 3. Types of Pronoun Objects

### A. Personal Pronouns
*   *Please tell **him** the news.*
*   *Give **it** to **me**.*

### B. Reflexive Pronouns
Used when Subject and Object are the same.
*   *I hurt **myself**.*
*   *She taught **herself** French.*

### C. Reciprocal Pronouns
Used for mutual action.
*   *They love **each other**.*
*   *We help **one another**.*

### D. Indefinite Pronouns
*   *I saw **someone**.*
*   *He bought **everything**.*

---

## 4. Common Errors
*   *Wrong:* He gave the book to **I**.
*   *Right:* He gave the book to **me**.
*   *Wrong:* Between you and **I**.
*   *Right:* Between you and **me**. (Object of preposition 'between').
