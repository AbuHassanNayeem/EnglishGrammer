## 1. Definition
A **Gerund** is the **-ing** form of a verb acting as a noun. When used as an object, it represents an activity or process.

*   *Form:* Verb + ing
*   *Function:* Direct Object

---

## 2. Examples
*   *I enjoy **swimming**.* (Object of 'enjoy').
*   *He quit **smoking**.* (Object of 'quit').
*   *She finished **reading**.* (Object of 'finished').

---

## 3. Verbs taking ONLY Gerund Objects
Some verbs generally cannot be followed by an Infinitive (to...).
*   *admit, avoid, consider, deny, enjoy, finish, imagine, keep, mind, miss, postpone, practice, quit, risk, stop, suggest.*

*   *Correct:* I avoid **eating** sugar.
*   *Wrong:* I avoid **to eat** sugar.

---

## 4. Complex Gerund Phrases
The object can be a full phrase involving the gerund.
*   *I DO NOT appreciate **[your talking during the movie]**.*
    *   *Head:* Talking.
    *   *Possessive:* Your (Formal usage prefers possessive before gerunds).
