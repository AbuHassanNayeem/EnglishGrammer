## 1. Definition
An **Infinitive Object** is the **To + Verb** form functioning as a noun object. It often expresses a desire, intent, or future plan.

*   *Form:* To + Base Verb
*   *Function:* Direct Object

---

## 2. Examples
*   *I want **to go**.*
*   *She decided **to stay**.*
*   *We plan **to visit**.*

---

## 3. Verbs taking ONLY Infinitive Objects
Some verbs generally cannot be followed by a Gerund.
*   *agree, ask, choose, decide, expect, help, hope, learn, manage, need, offer, plan, promise, refuse, seem, want, wish.*

*   *Correct:* I promise **to help**.
*   *Wrong:* I promise **helping**.

---

## 4. Gerund vs. Infinitive (Meaning Change)
Some verbs take both, but the meaning shifts.

### A. Stop
*   **Stop doing:** Quit the action forever. (*Stop smoking*).
*   **Stop to do:** Pause in order to do something. (*Stop walking **to smoke***).

### B. Remember
*   **Remember doing:** Recall a memory. (*I remember **locking** the door.*)
*   **Remember to do:** Don't forget a task. (*Remember **to lock** the door.*)

### C. Try
*   **Try doing:** Experiment with a method. (*Try **restarting** the computer.*)
*   **Try to do:** Make an effort (might fail). (*Try **to lift** the box.*)
