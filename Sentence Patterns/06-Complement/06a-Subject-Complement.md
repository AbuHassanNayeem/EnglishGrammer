## 1. Definition
A **Subject Complement** follows a **Linking Verb** (Copula) and identifies, renames, or describes the Subject.

*   *Usage:* Tells us more about the "Subject" rather than describing an action.
*   *Formula:* S + V + C

---

## 2. Two Main Types

### A. Predicate Nominative (Noun)
Renames the subject.
*   *He is **a doctor**.* (He = Doctor).
*   *She became **the winner**.*
*   *They remained **friends**.*

### B. Predicate Adjective (Adjective)
Describes the subject.
*   *He is **funny**.* (He has the quality of being funny).
*   *The soup tastes **salty**.*
*   *I feel **tired**.*

---

## 3. Linking Verbs (Copulas)
These verbs connect the subject to the compliment.

### True Linking Verbs (Always Linking)
*   **Be** (am, is, are, was, were)
*   **Become**
*   **Seem**

### Sensory Linking Verbs
*   **Look:** *You look happy.*
*   **Smell:** *It smells good.*
*   **Taste:** *It tastes sweet.*
*   **Feel:** *I feel sick.*
*   **Sound:** *That sounds great.*

### State/Change Verbs
*   **Remain/Stay:** *Stay calm.*
*   **Get/Grow/Turn:** *Get angry*, *Grow old*, *Turn red*.

---

## 4. Practice Exercises

### Identify the Subject Complement:
1. The winner is John.
2. The flowers smell lovely.
3. She became a lawyer.
4. He seems confused.

> **Answers:**
> 1. **John** (Noun - Predicate Nominative).
> 2. **Lovely** (Adjective - Predicate Adjective).
> 3. **A lawyer** (Noun - Predicate Nominative).
> 4. **Confused** (Adjective - Predicate Adjective).
