## 1. Definition
An **Object Complement** follows a Direct Object and renames or describes that Object. It occurs with **Complex-Transitive Verbs**.

*   *Formula:* S + V + O + C

---

## 2. Structure
The action of the verb causes the Object to become the Complement, or the verb expresses an opinion that the Object IS the Complement.

*   *They painted the door **red**.*
    *   (Action: Paint -> Result: Door is Red).
*   *I consider him **a genius**.*
    *   (Opinion: Him = Genius).

---

## 3. Common Verbs taking OC

### Verbs of Appointment / Naming
*   **Elect:** *Elect him president.*
*   **Name:** *Name her Sarah.*
*   **Call:** *Call me Ishmael.*
*   **Appoint:** *Appoint him manager.*
*   **Label:** *Label this dangerous.*

### Verbs of Opinion / Perception
*   **Consider:** *Consider it done.*
*   **Find:** *Find it difficult.*
*   **Think:** *Think him foolish.*
*   **Imagine:** *Imagine it possible.*

### Verbs of Result (Causative)
*   **Make:** *Make him happy.*
*   **Paint:** *Paint it black.*
*   **Keep:** *Keep it safe.*
*   **Leave:** *Leave the door open.*
*   **Get:** *Get it ready.*

---

## 4. Practice Exercises

### Identify O (Object) and C (Complement):
1. We made her captain.
2. He kept the house clean.
3. She called him a liar.

> **Answers:**
> 1. **O:** her | **C:** captain (Noun).
> 2. **O:** the house | **C:** clean (Adjective).
> 3. **O:** him | **C:** a liar (Noun).
