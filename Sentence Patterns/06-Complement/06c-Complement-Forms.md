## 1. Noun Phrase (Nominative)
Renames the subject or object.
*   *Subject C:* He is **my brother**.
*   *Object C:* They elected him **president**.

## 2. Adjective Phrase
Describes the subject or object.
*   *Subject C:* She is **very smart**.
*   *Object C:* He made her **angry**.

## 3. Prepositional Phrase
Functioning as an adjective.
*   *Subject C:* She is **in a hurry** (busy).
*   *Subject C:* This book is **of great value** (valuable).

## 4. Infinitive Phrase
Often used to define a plan or purpose.
*   *Subject C:* My goal is **to win**.
*   *Subject C:* To see is **to believe**.

## 5. Participle Phrase
*   *Subject C:* The situation is **worrying**. (Present Participle as Adj).
*   *Object C:* I found the dog **sleeping**.

## 6. Noun Clause
*   *Subject C:* The problem is **that we have no money**.
*   *Subject C:* The question is **who will go**.

## 7. Practice Exercises

### Identify the Form:
1. Her job is teaching math.
2. The box is heavy.
3. My wish is to travel.
4. The result was that he failed.

> **Answers:**
> 1. **Gerund Phrase** (Teaching math).
> 2. **Adjective** (Heavy).
> 3. **Infinitive Phrase** (To travel).
> 4. **Noun Clause** (That he failed).
