## 1. Introduction
Adverbials are classified by the **question they answer** (When? Where? How?).

---

## 2. Circumstantial Adverbials
These give details about the action.

### A. Place (Where?)
*   **Position:** Usually End.
*   *Examples:*
    *   *He lives **in London**.*
    *   *She arrived **here**.*
    *   *I looked **everywhere**.*

### B. Time (When?)
*   **Position:** End (neutral) or Front (emphasis).
*   *Examples:*
    *   *He left **yesterday**.*
    *   *I will see you **soon**.*
    *   ***Now** is the time.*

### C. Manner (How?)
*   **Position:** End (usual) or Mid (sometimes).
*   *Examples:*
    *   *She sings **beautifully**.*
    *   *He drove **fast**.*
    *   *They listened **with interest**.*

### D. Frequency (How Often?)
*   **Position:** Mid (between S and V) is most common.
*   *Examples:*
    *   *I **always** brush my teeth.*
    *   *She **never** lies.*
    *   *We visit **occasionally**.*

### E. Duration (How Long?)
*   **Position:** End.
*   *Examples:*
    *   *I waited **for two hours**.*
    *   *It rained **all day**.*

### F. Degree (How Much?)
*   **Position:** Mid (modifying verbs) or Before Adjective.
*   *Examples:*
    *   *I **totally** agree.*
    *   *It is **very** hot.*

---

## 3. Logical Adverbials
These connect ideas or show reasoning.

### A. Reason/Cause (Why?)
*   *He failed **because he didn't study**.*

### B. Purpose (What for?)
*   *I went **to buy milk**.*

### C. Condition (Under what circumstances?)
*   *I will go **if you go**.*

---

## 4. Practice Exercises

### Identify the Type:
1. She works **hard**.
2. We met **outside**.
3. He called **twice**.
4. I studied **to pass**.

> **Answers:**
> 1. **Manner** (How?)
> 2. **Place** (Where?)
> 3. **Frequency** (How often?)
> 4. **Purpose** (Why/What for?)

---

## 5. Quick Identification Tip ðŸ’¡
To find an adverbial, ask these questions to the verb:
*   **How?** (Manner)
*   **When?** (Time)
*   **Where?** (Place)
*   **Why?** (Reason/Purpose)
*   **To what extent?** (Degree)

If a phrase answers one of these, it is an **Adverbial**.
