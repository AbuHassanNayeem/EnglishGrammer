## 1. Introduction
"Adverbial" is a **function**. Many grammatical **forms** can act as adverbials.

---

## 2. Main Forms

### A. Adverb Phrase (AdvP)
The head is an adverb.
*   *He runs **fast**.* (Single word).
*   *He runs **very fast**.* (Intensifier + Adverb).

### B. Prepositional Phrase (PP)
The most common form for Place and Time.
*   *He is **in the kitchen**.*
*   *We met **on Sunday**.*
*   *She acted **with courage**.* (Manner).

### C. Noun Phrase (NP)
Some nouns indicate time without a preposition.
*   *I saw him **yesterday**.*
*   *We are going **home**.* (Home is a noun acting as adverbial).
*   *It weighs **five kilos**.*

### D. Clause (Finite)
A complete sentence structure acting as an adverb.
*   *I left **because I was tired**.*
*   *Call me **when you arrive**.*

### E. Infinitive Clause (Non-finite)
Expressing purpose.
*   *I went **to see him**.*

### F. Participle Clause (Non-finite)
*   ***Walking down the street**, I saw a dog.* (Time/Context).

---

## 3. Practice Exercises

### Identify the Form:
1. They arrived **last night**.
2. She spoke **loudly**.
3. He sat **on the floor**.
4. **If it rains**, we cancel.

> **Answers:**
> 1. **Noun Phrase** (Last night).
> 2. **Adverb Phrase** (Loudly).
> 3. **Prepositional Phrase** (On the floor).
> 4. **Finite Clause** (If it rains).
