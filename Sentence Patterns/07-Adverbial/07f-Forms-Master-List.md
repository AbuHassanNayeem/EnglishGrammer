## 1. Introduction
Adverbials can take many grammatical forms, ranging from single words to complete clauses.

## 2. Master Table of Forms

| Type | Example | Function/Meaning | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. Single-word Adverb** | *He runs **quickly**.* | Manner (How) | Can also indicate time (*yesterday*) or degree (*very*). |
| **2. Prepositional Phrase** | *She lives **in Dhaka**.* | Place (Where) | Also Time (*at noon*), Cause (*because of rain*). |
| **3. Noun Phrase (Time)** | *I saw him **last night**.* | Time (When) | Nouns acting as adverbs (Temporal expressions). |
| **4. Infinitive Phrase** | *He came **to help us**.* | Purpose (Why) | Answers "Why" or shows result. |
| **5. Adverbial Clause** | *I left **because it was late**.* | Reason/Cause | Full clause with Subject+Verb. |
| **6. Participial Phrase** | ***Walking fast**, he reached...* | Manner/Condition | Adds descriptive detail or cause. |
| **7. Conjunctive Adverb** | ***Therefore**, he left.* | Connection | Logical connector (Thus, However, Moreover). |

---

## 3. Multiple Functions
A sentence can combine multiple forms:
*   *She hurried **to the bus stop** (PP/Place) **in the morning** (PP/Time) **because she was late** (Clause/Reason).*
