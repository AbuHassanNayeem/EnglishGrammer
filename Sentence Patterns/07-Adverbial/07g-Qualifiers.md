# Qualifiers — Detailed Guide

## 1. Definition
**Qualifiers** (often called Intensifiers or Degree Adverbs) are words that modify or limit the **degree, intensity, or extent** of adjectives or other adverbs.
*   *Function:* They express "how much" or "to what extent" a quality exists.
*   *Positon:* Usually placed immediately **before** the word they modify.

---

## 2. Functions of Qualifiers

### A. Degree of Intensity
Shows how strong or weak a quality is.
*   *She is **extremely** intelligent.* (Modifies 'intelligent').
*   *He is **very** tall.*

### B. Limitation
Softens or restricts the meaning.
*   *He is **somewhat** tired.* (Limits the extent of 'tired').
*   *The movie was **fairly** good.*

### C. Precision
Makes descriptions more accurate.
*   *The train was **nearly** empty.* (Precise estimate).
*   *I am **almost** finished.*

---

## 3. Examples
*   **very** tall → "very" qualifies "tall" (Intensity).
*   **too** slowly → "too" qualifies "slowly" (Excess).
*   **quite** interesting → "quite" qualifies "interesting" (Moderate degree).
*   **almost** finished → "almost" qualifies "finished" (Partial completion).

---

## 4. Key Questions
To identify a qualifier, ask:
*   *To what extent?*
*   *How much?*
*   *In what way?*

---

## 5. Common Qualifiers List
*   **High Intensity:** Very, Extremely, Too, Entirely, Totally.
*   **Moderate/Low Intensity:** Quite, Rather, Fairly, Somewhat.
*   **Precision/Limit:** Almost, Nearly, Hardly, Barely, Enough.
