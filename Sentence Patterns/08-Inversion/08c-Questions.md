# Question Inversion (Interrogative) — Detailed Examples

## Pattern Structure

**Question Inversion:** Auxiliary/Modal + Subject + Main Verb (+ Other Elements)

In English questions, the auxiliary verb, modal verb, or a form of "be" moves before the subject, creating inversion. This is the most common type of inversion in everyday English.

---

## Yes/No Questions

Questions that can be answered with "yes" or "no."

### With "Be" Verb

**Present:**
1. Are you ready? (V: Are, S: you, C: ready)
   - Statement: You are ready.

2. Is she a teacher? (V: Is, S: she, C: a teacher)
   - Statement: She is a teacher.

3. Are they students? (V: Are, S: they, C: students)
   - Statement: They are students.

4. Is it cold? (V: Is, S: it, C: cold)
   - Statement: It is cold.

5. Am I late? (V: Am, S: I, C: late)
   - Statement: I am late.

6. Is the door open? (V: Is, S: the door, C: open)
   - Statement: The door is open.

7. Are the children happy? (V: Are, S: the children, C: happy)
   - Statement: The children are happy.

**Past:**
8. Was he there? (V: Was, S: he, A: there)
   - Statement: He was there.

9. Were they surprised? (V: Were, S: they, C: surprised)
   - Statement: They were surprised.

10. Was it difficult? (V: Was, S: it, C: difficult)
    - Statement: It was difficult.

11. Were you busy? (V: Were, S: you, C: busy)
    - Statement: You were busy.

## Wh-Questions (Information Questions)

Questions using wh-words to request specific information.

### What, Where, When, Who, Why, How

**What:**
55. What is your name? (Wh: What, V: is, S: your name)
56. What are you doing? (Wh: What, Aux: are, S: you, V: doing)
57. What did she say? (Wh: What, Aux: did, S: she, V: say)
58. What will happen? (Wh: What, Aux: will, V: happen)
59. What have you learned? (Wh: What, Aux: have, S: you, V: learned)

**Where:**
60. Where is the station? (Wh: Where, V: is, S: the station)
61. Where are you going? (Wh: Where, Aux: are, S: you, V: going)
62. Where did he go? (Wh: Where, Aux: did, S: he, V: go)
63. Where will she be? (Wh: Where, Aux: will, S: she, V: be)
64. Where have they gone? (Wh: Where, Aux: have, S: they, V: gone)

**When:**
65. When is the meeting? (Wh: When, V: is, S: the meeting)
66. When are you leaving? (Wh: When, Aux: are, S: you, V: leaving)
67. When did it happen? (Wh: When, Aux: did, S: it, V: happen)
68. When will he arrive? (Wh: When, Aux: will, S: he, V: arrive)
69. When have you been there? (Wh: When, Aux: have, S: you, V: been, A: there)

**Who:**
70. Who is that? (Wh: Who, V: is, S: that) — Who as subject complement
71. Who are you? (Wh: Who, V: are, S: you) — Who as subject complement
72. Who did you see? (Wh: Who, Aux: did, S: you, V: see) — Who as object
73. Who will come? (Wh: Who, Aux: will, V: come) — Who as subject
74. Who has finished? (Wh: Who, Aux: has, V: finished) — Who as subject

**Why:**
75. Why is she crying? (Wh: Why, Aux: is, S: she, V: crying)
76. Why are you late? (Wh: Why, V: are, S: you, C: late)
77. Why did he leave? (Wh: Why, Aux: did, S: he, V: leave)
78. Why will they come? (Wh: Why, Aux: will, S: they, V: come)
79. Why have you changed? (Wh: Why, Aux: have, S: you, V: changed)

**How:**
80. How is she? (Wh: How, V: is, S: she)
81. How are you doing? (Wh: How, Aux: are, S: you, V: doing)
82. How did it go? (Wh: How, Aux: did, S: it, V: go)
83. How will we know? (Wh: How, Aux: will, S: we, V: know)
84. How have things been? (Wh: How, Aux: have, S: things, V: been)

**How much/many:**
85. How much is it? (Wh: How much, V: is, S: it)
86. How many are there? (Wh: How many, V: are, Exp: there)
87. How many did you buy? (Wh: How many, Aux: did, S: you, V: buy)

**Which:**
88. Which is yours? (Wh: Which, V: is, S: yours)
89. Which one do you want? (Wh: Which one, Aux: do, S: you, V: want)
90. Which way did he go? (Wh: Which way, Aux: did, S: he, V: go)

**Whose:**
91. Whose is this? (Wh: Whose, V: is, S: this)
92. Whose book is it? (Wh: Whose book, V: is, S: it)
93. Whose car did you borrow? (Wh: Whose car, Aux: did, S: you, V: borrow)

## Tag Questions

Short questions added to statements to confirm information.

**Positive Statement → Negative Tag:**
94. You are coming, aren't you? (S: you, V: are, Tag: aren't you)
95. She likes pizza, doesn't she? (S: she, V: likes, Tag: doesn't she)
96. They will help, won't they? (S: they, Aux: will, Tag: won't they)
97. He can swim, can't he? (S: he, Aux: can, Tag: can't he)
98. You have finished, haven't you? (S: you, Aux: have, Tag: haven't you)

**Negative Statement → Positive Tag:**
99. You aren't tired, are you? (S: you, V: aren't, Tag: are you)
100. She doesn't smoke, does she? (S: she, Aux: doesn't, Tag: does she)
101. They won't be late, will they? (S: they, Aux: won't, Tag: will they)
102. He can't drive, can he? (S: he, Aux: can't, Tag: can he)
103. You haven't seen him, have you? (S: you, Aux: haven't, Tag: have you)

## Special Cases

### Subject-Auxiliary Inversion vs. No Inversion

**When "Who/What" is the Subject (NO Inversion):**
104. Who came? (S: Who, V: came) — NO inversion
105. What happened? (S: What, V: happened) — NO inversion
106. Who is calling? (S: Who, Aux: is, V: calling) — NO inversion

**When "Who/What" is NOT the Subject (Inversion):**
107. Who did you see? (Wh: Who, Aux: did, S: you, V: see) — inversion
108. What are you doing? (Wh: What, Aux: are, S: you, V: doing) — inversion

## Indirect Questions (NO Inversion)

In indirect/embedded questions, normal word order returns:

**Direct Question:**
116. Where is he? (inversion)

**Indirect Question:**
117. I wonder where he is. (NO inversion)
118. Do you know where he is? (NO inversion in embedded clause)
119. She asked where he was. (NO inversion)
120. Tell me what you want. (NO inversion)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ❌ Double Inversion
- **Wrong:** Where did she went? ✗
- **Correct:** Where did she go? ✓

### ❌ No Auxiliary with Main Verb
- **Wrong:** Likes she coffee? ✗
- **Correct:** Does she like coffee? ✓

### ❌ Inversion in Indirect Questions
- **Wrong:** I wonder where is he. ✗
- **Correct:** I wonder where he is. ✓

### ❌ Wrong Auxiliary in Questions
- **Wrong:** Did she can swim? ✗
- **Correct:** Could she swim? ✓

---

## Summary

**Question Inversion** is essential for forming questions:

✅ Auxiliary/Modal + Subject + Main Verb  
✅ Most common type of inversion in English  
✅ Used in yes/no questions, wh-questions, tag questions  
✅ NO inversion when wh-word is the subject  
✅ NO inversion in indirect/embedded questions  
✅ Negative questions use negative auxiliary first  

**Key Rule:** In direct questions, put the auxiliary/modal before the subject!
