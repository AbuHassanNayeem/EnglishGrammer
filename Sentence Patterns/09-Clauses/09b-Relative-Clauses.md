## 1. Definition
A **Relative Clause** modifies a Noun or Noun Phrase. It usually follows the noun immediately.
*   *The man **who called me** is here.*

---

## 2. Relative Pronouns

| Pronoun | Use | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Who** | People (Subject) | The man **who** sat down. |
| **Whom** | People (Object) | The man **whom** I met. |
| **Whose** | Possession | The man **whose** car was stolen. |
| **Which** | Things | The book **which** I read. |
| **That** | People/Things (Restrictive) | The book **that** I read. |

---

## 3. Restrictive vs. Non-Restrictive

### A. Restrictive (Defining)
Essential information. Identifies *which one*. **NO COMMAS.**
*   *The student **who works hard** will succeed.*
    *   (Only the hard-working ones. Not all students).
*   *Use **That** or **Which/Who**.*

### B. Non-Restrictive (Non-Defining)
Extra information. **COMMAS REQUIRED.**
*   *My father, **who works hard**, is tired.*
    *   (I only have one father; the clause just adds info).
*   *Use **Which/Who**. NEVER use **That**.*
    *   *Wrong:* My father, that works hard...

---

## 4. Omitting the Pronoun (Reduced Clauses)
You can delete the pronoun if it is the **Object** of the clause.
*   *Full:* The book **that** I read.
*   *Reduced:* The book I read. (Common and correct).

You CANNOT delete if it is the Subject.
*   *Full:* The man **who** came.
*   *Reduced:* The man came? (No, this changes meaning to a main clause).

---

## 5. Identifying Prepositions
Formal English puts the preposition before "Whom/Which".
*   *Formal:* The house **in which** I live.
*   *Informal:* The house **which** I live **in**.
*   *Informal:* The house **that** I live **in**.
*   *Very Informal:* The house I live **in**.

---

## 6. Practice Exercises

### Correct or Incorrect?
1. The car, that I bought, is red.
2. The person who I talked to was nice.
3. The person I talked to was nice.

> **Answers:**
> 1. **Incorrect.** (Non-restrictive commas cannot use 'that'. Must be 'which').
> 2. **Correct.** (Informal 'who' for object).
> 3. **Correct.** (Omitted pronoun).
