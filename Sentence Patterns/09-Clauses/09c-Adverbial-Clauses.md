## 1. Definition
An **Adverbial Clause** modifies the main verb. It answers When, Why, How, or Under What Condition. It always starts with a **Subordinating Conjunction**.

---

## 2. Common Types

### A. Time
*   *Markers:* When, While, Before, After, As soon as, Since.
*   *Example:* *Call me **when you arrive**.*

### B. Reason/Cause
*   *Markers:* Because, Since, As, Given that.
*   *Example:* *I slept **because I was tired**.*

### C. Condition
*   *Markers:* If, Unless, Provided that, As long as.
*   *Example:* ***If it rains**, we will cancel.*

### D. Concession (Contrast)
*   *Markers:* Although, Though, Even though, While, Whereas.
*   *Example:* ***Although it rained**, we played.*

### E. Purpose
*   *Markers:* So that, In order that.
*   *Example:* *I study **so that I can pass**.*

### F. Result
*   *Markers:* So... that, Such... that.
*   *Example:* *He was **so** tired **that he slept**.*

---

## 3. Punctuation Rule (The Comma)
*   **If the clause comes FIRST:** Use a comma.
    *   *Because he was late**,** he missed the bus.*
*   **If the clause comes LAST:** usually NO comma.
    *   *He missed the bus because he was late.*
    *   *Exception:* Concession clauses often take a comma even at the end (*I went, although I was tired*).

---

## 4. Reducing Adverbial Clauses
If the Subject is the same in both clauses, you can reduce it to a phrase.
*   *Full:* ***While I was** walking*, I fell.
*   *Reduced:* ***While walking**, I fell.* (Or just *Walking down the street...*)
*   *Wrong:* ***While walking**, the rain started.* (Dangling Modifier! The rain was not walking).

---

## 5. Practice Exercises

### Punctuate Correctly:
1. If you go I will go.
2. I will go if you go.

> **Answers:**
> 1. If you go**,** I will go.
> 2. I will go if you go. (No comma).
