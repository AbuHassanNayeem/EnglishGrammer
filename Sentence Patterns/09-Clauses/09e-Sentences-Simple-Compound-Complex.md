## 1. Simple Sentence
**One Independent Clause.**
*   *Subject + Verb.*
*   *Birds fly.*
*   *The big red dog ran quickly home.* (Still simple! Just one verb linkage).

## 2. Compound Sentence
**Two Independent Clauses** joined by:
1.  **Coordinator (FANBOYS):** For, And, Nor, But, Or, Yet, So.
2.  **Semicolon (;)**
3.  **Conjunctive Adverb:** *; however,* / *; therefore,*

*   *I like tea, **and** he likes coffee.*
*   *I like tea; he likes coffee.*

## 3. Complex Sentence
**One Independent + One Dependent Clause.**
*   *I will go **if you go**.*
*   ***Because he was late**, he ran.*
*   *The man **who called** is here.*

## 4. Compound-Complex Sentence
**Two Independent + One Dependent Clause.**
*   *I like tea, **and** he likes coffee **because it wakes him up**.*
    *   Indep 1: I like tea.
    *   Indep 2: He likes coffee.
    *   Dep 1: Because it wakes him up.

---

## 5. Structure & Logic
*   **Coordination (Compound):** Suggests equality. A and B are both important.
*   **Subordination (Complex):** Suggests hierarchy. Main idea is the Independent clause; the Dependent clause provides background/reason/time.

---

## 6. Practice Exercises

### Identify the Sentence Type:
1. The rain stopped.
2. The rain stopped, and the sun came out.
3. The rain stopped because the wind changed.
4. Although it rained, we played, and we won.

> **Answers:**
> 1. **Simple**
> 2. **Compound**
> 3. **Complex**
> 4. **Compound-Complex** (Dep: Although... + Indep: We played + Indep: We won).
