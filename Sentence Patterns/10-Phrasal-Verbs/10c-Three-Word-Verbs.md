## 1. Definition
These are **Phrasal-Prepositional Verbs**. They consists of:
**Verb + Particle + Preposition**

*   *I look forward to it.*
*   Structure: S + V + Part + Prep + O

---

## 2. Characteristics
1.  **Always Transitive:** They generally require an object.
2.  **Always Inseparable:** You can NEVER separate the parts.
    *   *Wrong:* I look forward it to.
    *   *Right:* I look forward to it.

---

## 3. Common List

| Verb | Meaning | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **come up with** | Invent/Produce | *She came up with a great idea.* |
| **look forward to** | Anticipate | *I look forward to meeting you.* |
| **get along with** | Have harmony | *I get along with my boss.* |
| **put up with** | Tolerate | *I can't put up with this noise.* |
| **run out of** | Deplete | *We ran out of coffee.* |
| **check up on** | Verify/Monitor | *The doctor checked up on the patient.* |
| **face up to** | Accept difficult truth | *He must face up to reality.* |
| **cut down on** | Reduce consumption | *You should cut down on sugar.* |
| **get away with** | Escape punishment | *He got away with the crime.* |
| **look down on** | Feel superior to | *She looks down on us.* |
| **look up to** | Admire/Respect | *I look up to my father.* |

---

## 4. Practice Exercises

### Correct the sentence:
1. He ran out milk of.
2. She puts up with him.
3. I look forward seeing you.

> **Answers:**
> 1. **Wrong:** *He ran out of milk.* (Inseparable).
> 2. **Correct.** (Puts up with = Tolerates context).
> 3. **Wrong:** *I look forward **to** seeing you.* (Missing the preposition 'to', followed by Gerund).
