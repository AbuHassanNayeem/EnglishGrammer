## 1. Subjects Joined by "AND"
**Rule:** Usually **Plural**.
*   ***Tom and Jerry** **are** funny.*

**Exception:** If they form a **single unit/concept**, use **Singular**.
*   ***Peanut butter and jelly** **is** my favorite.*
*   ***Bed and breakfast** **costs** $50.*
*   ***The Founder and CEO** **is** here.* (One person holds both titles).
    *   *Compare:* *The Founder and the CEO **are** here.* (Two different people).

---

## 2. Subjects Joined by "OR" / "NOR" (Proximity Rule)
**Rule:** The Verb agrees with the **Closest Subject**.

### A. Singular + Singular
*   *Neither **John nor Mary** **is** home.*

### B. Plural + Plural
*   *Neither **the cats nor the dogs** **are** hungry.*

### C. Mixed (Singular + Plural)
*   *Either the **boy** or his **friends** **are** coming.* (Friends is closer).
*   *Either his **friends** or the **boy** **is** coming.* (Boy is closer).

---

## 3. "Both...And"
**Rule:** Always **Plural**.
*   *Both John and Mary **are** coming.*

---

## 4. Practice Exercises

### Choose the Correct Verb:
1. Fish and chips (is/are) delicious.
2. Neither the players nor the coach (like/likes) the ruling.
3. The coach or the players (is/are) responsible.

> **Answers:**
> 1. **is** (Single dish concept).
> 2. **likes** (Coach is closer).
> 3. **are** (Players is closer).
