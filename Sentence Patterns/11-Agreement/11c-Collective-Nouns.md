## 1. Collective Nouns (Group Words)
Words like *Team, Family, Jury, Committee, Class, Audience, Staff*.

### Rule: Context Matters
*   **Singular:** If the group acts in **unison** (as one body).
    *   *The jury **has** reached a verdict.*
*   **Plural:** If the members act **individually**.
    *   *The jury **are** arguing among themselves.*

*(Note: British English prefers Plural for collectives; American English prefers Singular).*

---

## 2. Expressions of Quantity
*   **Money/Time/Distance:** Usually **Singular** (seen as one amount).
    *   *Ten dollars **is** too much.*
    *   *Five miles **is** a long walk.*
    *   *Two weeks **was** enough.*
*   **Fractions/Percentages:** Look at the "Of" phrase.
    *   *Half of the **pie is** gone.* (Pie = Single).
    *   *Half of the **pies are** gone.* (Pies = Plural).

---

## 3. The "-ics" Words
Words ending in 's' that are singular.
*   *News:* *The **news is** bad.* (Always singular).
*   *Subjects:* *Physics, Mathematics, Economics, Politics* -> **Singular**.
    *   *Mathematics **is** hard.*
    *   *Exception:* When meaning "opinions" or "calculations": *His politics **are** strange.*

---

## 4. Practice Exercises

### Choose the Correct Verb:
1. The family (is/are) eating dinner.
2. Fifty percent of the book (is/are) boring.
3. Fifty percent of the pages (is/are) torn.

> **Answers:**
> 1. **is** (One unit).
> 2. **is** (Book is singular).
> 3. **are** (Pages are plural).
