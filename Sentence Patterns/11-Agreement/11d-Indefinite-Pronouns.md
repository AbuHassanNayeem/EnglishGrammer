## 1. Always Singular
Use a singular verb, even if they imply multiple people.
*   **List:** *Each, Every, Either, Neither, One, Everyone, Everybody, Everything, Anyone, Anybody, Anything, No one, Nobody, Nothing, Someone, Somebody, Something.*

*   ***Each** of the boys **has** a pen.* (Not *have*).
*   ***Everyone** **loves** pizza.*

## 2. Always Plural
*   **List:** *Both, Few, Many, Several.*

*   ***Both** **are** happy.*
*   ***Many** **were** lost.*

## 3. Variable (Singular or Plural)
Depends on the noun they refer to (look at the "of" phrase).
*   **List:** *All, Any, More, Most, None, Some.* (Mnemonic: SAMMAN).

*   *All of the **water** **is** gone.* (Uncountable).
*   *All of the **cups** **are** gone.* (Countable Plural).
*   *None of the **pie** **was** eaten.*
*   *None of the **students** **were** correct.*

---

## 4. "None" - Special Note
Strict grammar says "None" (Not one) is singular (*None is*).
Modern usage allows "None" to be plural (*None are*) depending on emphasis.
*   *Is there any milk? None **is** left.*
*   *Are there any cookies? None **are** left.*

---

## 5. Practice Exercises

### Choose the Correct Verb:
1. Each of the cars (was/were) fast.
2. Some of the food (is/are) cold.
3. Some of the fries (is/are) cold.

> **Answers:**
> 1. **was** (Each is always singular).
> 2. **is** (Food is uncountable).
> 3. **are** (Fries are plural).
