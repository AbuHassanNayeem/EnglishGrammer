## 1. Relative Clauses
**Rule:** The verb in the clause agrees with the **Antecedent** (the word before *who/which/that*).

*   *He is the **man** who **writes** well.* (Man -> Writes).
*   *They are the **men** who **write** well.* (Men -> Write).

**The Tricky "One of those..." Construction:**
*   *He is **one** of those **people** who **complain**.*
    *   (Antecedent is *People* -> Plural verb *Complain*).
    *   Logic: Of those people who complain, he is one.
*   *He is the **only one** of those people who **complains**.*
    *   (Antecedent is *One* -> Singular verb *Complains*).

---

## 2. Complement Disagreement
**Rule:** The verb agrees with the **Subject**, NOT the Subject Complement.

*   *My **favorite fruit** **is** apples.*
    *   (Subject: Fruit [Singular] -> Verb: Is).
*   ***Apples** **are** my favorite fruit.*
    *   (Subject: Apples [Plural] -> Verb: Are).

---

## 3. Titles and Names
**Rule:** Titles of works are singular.

*   ***Star Wars** **is** a great movie.*
*   ***The United States** **has** a larger population.*

---

## 4. Practice Exercises

### Choose the Correct Verb:
1. She is one of those students who (study/studies) hard.
2. The primary cause of accidents (is/are) cell phones.

> **Answers:**
> 1. **study** (Antecedent: Students -> Plural).
> 2. **is** (Subject: Cause -> Singular. "Cell phones" is the complement).
