## 1. Definition
A **Vocative** is a noun, proper name, title, or term of address used to **directly call or address** someone.
*   *Function:* Identifies the person being spoken to.
*   *Grammar:* It is **extra-grammatical** (not Subject, Object, or Verb). Removing it leaves the sentence intact.

---

## 2. Key Features
1.  **Direct Address:** Always addresses someone directly.
2.  **Punctuation:** Always separated by **commas** (or exclamation marks).
3.  **Independence:** Does not affect the verb agreement or sentence structure.
    *   *John, please sit down.* -> *Please sit down.* (Still correct).

---

## 3. Positions and Rules
Vocatives can appear anywhere, but punctuation must isolate them.

### A. Beginning (Front)
*   ***John**, come here!*
*   ***Sir**, your table is ready.*

### B. Middle (Medial)
*   *Could you, **Sarah**, pass the salt?*
*   *I think, **my friend**, you are wrong.*

### C. End (Final)
*   *Can you help me, **Mom**?*
*   *Welcome to the show, **Ladies and Gentlemen**.*

---

## 4. Purpose of Vocatives
*   **Gain Attention:** *Hey, everyone, listen!*
*   **Show Respect:** *Doctor, I have a question.*
*   **Express Emotion:** *Oh, John, I can't believe it!*
*   **Address Groups:** *Team, let's go!*
