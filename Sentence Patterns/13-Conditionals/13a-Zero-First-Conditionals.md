# Zero & First Conditionals — Detailed Guide

## 1. Zero Conditional (General Truths)

### Structure
**If + Present Simple, Present Simple**

### Usage
Expresses scientific facts, general truths, or habitual actions.

### Examples
- *If water reaches 100°C, it **boils**.*
- *If you **heat** ice, it **melts**.*
- *If I **wake** up late, I **miss** the bus.*

### Key Point
Both clauses use **present tense** because the result is always true.

---

## 2. First Conditional (Real Future)

### Structure
**If + Present Simple, will + Base Verb**

### Usage
Expresses real, likely future events and their results.

### Examples
- *If it **rains** tomorrow, I **will stay** home.*
- *If you **study** hard, you **will pass** the exam.*
- *If she **calls**, I **will answer**.*

### Variations
- **Unless = If not:** *Unless you hurry, you'll be late.* (= If you don't hurry...)
- **Imperative:** *If you see John, tell him to call me.*
- **May/Might:** *If it rains, we might cancel.* (less certain)

---

## 3. Time Clauses (Similar Pattern)
When, As soon as, Before, After, Until also use present tense for future:
- *When I **get** home, I **will** call you.* (NOT "will get")

---

## 4. Practice Exercises

### Complete the sentences:
1. If water ______ (freeze), it ______ (become) ice.
2. If you ______ (touch) fire, you ______ (get) burned.
3. If it ______ (rain) tomorrow, we ______ (cancel) the picnic.

> **Answers:**
> 1. freezes, becomes (Zero)
> 2. touch, get (Zero)
> 3. rains, will cancel (First)
