# Mixed Conditionals — Detailed Guide

## 1. Definition
**Mixed Conditionals** combine different time references in the IF clause and the main clause. They express complex cause-effect relationships across time periods.

---

## 2. Type 1: Past Condition → Present Result

### Structure
**If + Past Perfect, would + Base Verb**

### Usage
A past action/event has a present consequence.

### Examples
- *If I **had studied** medicine, I **would be** a doctor now.*
  - (I didn't study medicine in the past → I'm not a doctor now)
- *If you **had saved** money, you **wouldn't be** broke.*
  - (You didn't save → You are broke now)
- *If we **had left** on time, we **would be** there by now.*

---

## 3. Type 2: Present Condition → Past Result

### Structure
**If + Past Simple, would have + Past Participle**

### Usage
A present/ongoing situation affected a past result.

### Examples
- *If I **were** more careful, I **wouldn't have made** that mistake.*
  - (I'm not careful → I made a mistake)
- *If she **liked** him, she **would have accepted** his invitation.*
  - (She doesn't like him → She didn't accept)

---

## 4. Comparison Table

| Type | IF Clause | Main Clause | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **Standard 2nd** | Past | would + base | *If I were rich, I would travel.* |
| **Standard 3rd** | Past Perfect | would have + PP | *If I had known, I would have helped.* |
| **Mixed (Past→Present)** | Past Perfect | would + base | *If I had slept well, I would feel better.* |
| **Mixed (Present→Past)** | Past | would have + PP | *If I were smarter, I would have solved it.* |

---

## 5. Practice Exercises

### Identify if standard or mixed, then complete:
1. If I ______ (win) the lottery last year, I ______ (be) rich now.
2. If he ______ (be) a good driver, he ______ (not crash) yesterday.

> **Answers:**
> 1. had won, would be (Mixed: past→present)
> 2. were, wouldn't have crashed (Mixed: present→past)
