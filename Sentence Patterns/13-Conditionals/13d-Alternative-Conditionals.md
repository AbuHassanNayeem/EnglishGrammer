# Alternative Conditionals — Detailed Guide

## 1. Unless (If not)

### Structure
**Unless + Present, will/won't**

### Meaning
Unless = If not (but more emphatic)

### Examples
- *Unless you **hurry**, you'll be late.* (= If you don't hurry)
- *I won't go unless you **come** with me.* (= If you don't come)

### Watch Out
Don't use double negatives:
- ❌ *Unless you don't study...*
- ✅ *Unless you study...*

---

## 2. Provided that / As long as (On condition that)

### Usage
Emphasizes the condition is necessary.

### Examples
- *You can borrow my car **provided that** you drive carefully.*
- *I'll help you **as long as** you promise to study.*
- *We'll go **on condition that** the weather is good.*

---

## 3. In case (Precaution)

### Usage
Prepare for a possible situation.

### Examples
- *Take an umbrella **in case it rains**.* (It might rain, be prepared)
- *I'll bring extra money **in case** we need it.*

### vs. If
- **If:** Condition determines action
  - *If it rains, I'll take an umbrella.* (Then I'll take it)
- **In case:** Preparation for possibility
  - *I'll take an umbrella in case it rains.* (I'm taking it now as precaution)

---

## 4. Otherwise (Or else)

### Usage
Shows negative consequence if condition isn't met.

### Examples
- *Hurry up; **otherwise**, you'll be late.*
- *Study hard; **otherwise**, you won't pass.*

---

## 5. Inversion in Conditionals (Formal)

### Should (First Conditional)
- *Standard:* If you **should** need help, call me.
- *Formal:* **Should** you need help, call me.

### Were (Second Conditional)
- *Standard:* If I **were** rich...
- *Formal:* **Were** I rich... (see [08-Inversion](Inversion.md))

### Had (Third Conditional)
- *Standard:* If I **had** known...
- *Formal:* **Had** I known...

---

## 6. Practice Exercises

### Rewrite using the word in brackets:
1. If you don't apologize, I won't forgive you. (unless)
2. If it rains, take this umbrella. (in case)

> **Answers:**
> 1. Unless you apologize, I won't forgive you.
> 2. Take this umbrella in case it rains.
