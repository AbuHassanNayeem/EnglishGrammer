## 1. Introduction
**Conditional sentences** express hypothetical situations and their consequences. They show cause-effect relationships across different time periods and levels of reality.

This chapter covers all conditional types from factual to impossible.

## 3. Quick Reference

| Type | Time | Structure | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **Zero** | Always true | If + Present, Present | *If you heat water, it boils.* |
| **First** | Real future | If + Present, will | *If it rains, I'll stay home.* |
| **Second** | Hypothetical present | If + Past, would | *If I were rich, I'd travel.* |
| **Third** | Hypothetical past | If + Past Perfect, would have | *If I had known, I'd have helped.* |
| **Mixed** | Cross-time | Various | *If I had studied, I'd be a doctor now.* |
