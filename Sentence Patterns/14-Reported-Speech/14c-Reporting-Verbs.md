# Reporting Verbs — Detailed Guide

## 1. Say vs Tell

### Say (No indirect object)
**Structure:** say + (that) + clause

**Examples:**
- *He **said** (that) he was tired.*
- *She **said** "Hello."*

**Note:** Don't use "say + person":
- ❌ *He said me...*
- ✅ *He said to me...*

### Tell (Requires indirect object)
**Structure:** tell + person + (that) + clause

**Examples:**
- *He **told me** (that) he was tired.*
- *She **told him** to leave.*

**Note:** Always needs "tell + someone":
- ❌ *He told that...*
- ✅ *He told me that...*

## 2. Reporting Verbs by Pattern

### A. Verb + that-clause
*   **say, tell, mention, explain, announce, claim, admit, deny, promise, suggest**

**Examples:**
- *She **mentioned** (that) she was leaving.*
- *He **admitted** (that) he had lied.*
- *They **promised** (that) they would come.*

### B. Verb + infinitive
*   **agree, refuse, offer, threaten, promise, decide**

**Examples:**
- *She **agreed** to help.*
- *He **refused** to go.*
- *They **offered** to pay.*

### C. Verb + object + infinitive
*   **tell, ask, order, advise, warn, remind, persuade, invite**

**Examples:**
- *She **told me** to wait.*
- *He **advised us** to leave.*
- *They **invited me** to come.*

### D. Verb + gerund
*   **suggest, recommend, admit, deny**

**Examples:**
- *She **suggested** going home.*
- *He **admitted** stealing the money.*
- *They **denied** knowing him.*

### E. Verb + preposition + gerund
*   **apologize for, insist on, complain about, accuse...of**

**Examples:**
- *She **apologized for** being late.*
- *He **insisted on** paying.*
- *They **accused him of** lying.*

---

## 3. Common Reporting Verbs

| Verb | Pattern | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **say** | that-clause | *He said (that) he was tired.* |
| **tell** | person + that/to | *She told me (that) she would come.* |
| **ask** | person + to / if/whether | *He asked me to wait.* |
| **order** | person + to | *She ordered him to leave.* |
| **promise** | that / to | *He promised to help.* |
| **suggest** | that / gerund | *She suggested going home.* |
| **advise** | person + to | *He advised me to study.* |
| **warn** | person + (not) to | *She warned us not to go.* |
| **admit** | that / gerund | *He admitted (that) he had lied.* |
| **deny** | that / gerund | *She denied stealing it.* |
| **apologize** | for + gerund | *He apologized for being late.* |
| **insist** | on + gerund / that | *She insisted on coming.* |

## 4. Practice Exercises

### Choose the correct reporting verb pattern:
1. "I'm sorry I'm late." → She ______ being late.
2. "You should study harder." → He ______ me ______ study harder.
3. "I will help you." → She ______ to help me.

> **Answers:**
> 1. apologized for
> 2. advised, to
> 3. promised / agreed
