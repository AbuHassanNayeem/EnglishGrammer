## 1. Definition
**Addition connectors** add information or ideas to what was previously stated.

---

## 2. Common Addition Connectors

### Furthermore / Moreover (Formal)
**Meaning:** Adding reinforcing information

**Examples:**
- *The hotel was expensive. **Furthermore**, the service was poor.*
- *The project is behind schedule. **Moreover**, we are over budget.*

### In addition / Additionally (Formal)
**Meaning:** Adding extra information

**Examples:**
- *He speaks French. **In addition**, he speaks German.*
- *The car is fuel-efficient. **Additionally**, it's very reliable.*

### Also / As well (Neutral)
**Meaning:** Simple addition

**Examples:**
- *She is smart. She is **also** kind.*
- *I like coffee. I like tea **as well**.*

### Besides (Informal)
**Meaning:** Adding a reason/extra point

**Examples:**
- *I don't want to go. **Besides**, I'm too tired.*
- *The movie was boring. **Besides**, it was too long.*

---

## 3. Position & Punctuation

### Sentence-Initial (Most Common)
Use a **comma** after the connector:
- *I'm tired. **Furthermore,** I have a headache.*
- *She's late. **Moreover,** she forgot the files.*

### Mid-Sentence (Also)
- *He is **also** a talented musician.*
- *She can **also** speak Japanese.*

### Sentence-Final (As well / Too)
- *I want to come **as well**.*
- *He speaks French, **too**.*

---

## 4. Formality Scale

| Formal | Neutral | Informal |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Furthermore | Also | Besides |
| Moreover | In addition | Plus |
| Additionally | As well | And |

## 5. Practice Exercises

### Choose the appropriate connector:
1. The exam was difficult. ______, I didn't have enough time.
2. She is intelligent. ______, she is very hardworking.

> **Answers:**
> 1. Furthermore/Moreover/Besides (any works)
> 2. Also/Additionally/In addition
