# Contrast Connectors — Detailed Guide

## 1. Definition
**Contrast connectors** show opposition, unexpected results, or differences between ideas.

---

## 2. Conjunctive Adverbs (Between Sentences)

### However (Formal Contrast)
**Meaning:** Shows contrast between two statements

**Punctuation:** Semicolon OR period + comma
- *The hotel was expensive**; however,** it was worth it.*
- *The hotel was expensive. **However,** it was worth it.*

### Nevertheless / Nonetheless (Strong Contrast)
**Meaning:** Despite what was just said, this is still true

**Examples:**
- *The exam was difficult. **Nevertheless**, I passed.*
- *He's rude. **Nonetheless**, he's a skilled worker.*

### On the other hand (Alternative Perspective)
**Meaning:** Presenting another side

**Examples:**
- *The city is exciting. **On the other hand**, it's very expensive.*
- *Online shopping is convenient. **On the other hand**, you can't try things on.*

### In contrast / By contrast (Comparison)
**Meaning:** Highlighting a difference

**Examples:**
- *John is tall. **In contrast**, his brother is quite short.*
- *Summer is hot. **By contrast**, winter is freezing.*

---

## 3. Subordinating Conjunctions (Within Sentences)

### Although / Though / Even though
**Position:** Beginning or middle

**Examples:**
- ***Although** it was raining, we went out.*
- *We went out **although** it was raining.*
- ***Even though** he studied, he failed.* (stronger emphasis)

### While / Whereas (Comparison)
**Examples:**
- ***While** I like coffee, my sister prefers tea.*
- *He is tall, **whereas** his brother is short.*

---

## 4. Prepositions (+ Noun/Gerund)

### Despite / In spite of
**Structure:** Despite/In spite of + noun/gerund

**Examples:**
- ***Despite** the rain, we went out.* (NOT "despite of")
- ***In spite of** studying hard, he failed.*
- ***Despite** being tired, she continued working.*

### Comparison with Although
- ✅ ***Despite** the rain**, we went out. (+ noun)
- ✅ ***Although it was raining**, we went out. (+ clause)
- ❌ *Despite it was raining...* (Wrong!)

---

## 5. Summary Table

| Type | Connector | Structure | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **Adverb** | However | Sentence. However, | *It's cold. However, it's sunny.* |
| **Adverb** | Nevertheless | Sentence. Nevertheless, | *It's late. Nevertheless, I'll help.* |
| **Conjunction** | Although | Although + clause | *Although it rained, we went out.* |
| **Conjunction** | While | While + clause | *While I agree, I have concerns.* |
| **Preposition** | Despite | Despite + noun/gerund | *Despite the rain, we went out.* |

---

## 6. Common Mistakes

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Correct |
|:---|:---|
| *However it was late...* | *However, it was late...* (comma!) |
| *Despite of the rain...* | *Despite the rain...* (no "of") |
| *Despite it was raining...* | *Despite **the rain**/it **being** rainy...* |

---

## 7. Practice Exercises

### Choose the correct connector:
1. It was expensive. ______, I bought it.
2. ______ being tired, she continued working.

> **Answers:**
> 1. However/Nevertheless/Nonetheless
> 2. Despite/In spite of
