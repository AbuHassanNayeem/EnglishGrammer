# Result & Cause Connectors — Detailed Guide

## 1. Result Connectors

Show that something is a **consequence** of a previous statement.

### Therefore (Formal)
**Meaning:** For this reason / As a logical result

**Examples:**
- *It was raining. **Therefore**, we stayed home.*
- *He didn't study. **Therefore**, he failed.*

### Thus / Hence (Very Formal)
**Meaning:** Same as Therefore (academic writing)

**Examples:**
- *The experiment failed. **Thus**, we need to repeat it.*
- *There is no evidence. **Hence**, we cannot conclude anything.*

### Consequently / As a result (Formal)
**Meaning:** As a consequence

**Examples:**
- *The roads were icy. **Consequently**, many accidents occurred.*
- *Sales decreased. **As a result**, the company made layoffs.*

### So (Informal - Conjunction)
**Meaning:** Simple result

**Structure:** Sentence**,** so sentence. (No period before "so")

**Examples:**
- *It was raining**, so** we stayed home.*
- *I was tired**, so** I went to bed early.*

---

## 2. Cause/Reason Connectors

Show that something is the **reason** for a result.

### Because / Since / As (Conjunctions)
**Structure:** Because/Since/As + clause

**Examples:**
- *We stayed home **because** it was raining.*
- ***Since** you're here, let me ask you something.*
- ***As** I was tired, I went to bed.*

### Because of / Due to / Owing to (Prepositions)
**Structure:** Because of/Due to/Owing to + noun/gerund

**Examples:**
- *We stayed home **because of** the rain.* (NOT "because of it was raining")
- *The match was cancelled **due to** bad weather.*
- ***Owing to** technical problems, the flight was delayed.*

### Therefore vs Because
- **Result:** *It rained. **Therefore**, we stayed home.* (Rain → Stay)
- **Cause:** *We stayed home **because** it rained.* (Same meaning, different structure)

---

## 3. Summary Table

| Type | Connector | Structure | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **Result** | Therefore | Sentence. Therefore, | *It rained. Therefore, we stayed.* |
| **Result** | Thus/Hence | Sentence. Thus/Hence, | *He's ill. Thus, he can't come.* |
| **Result** | So | Sentence, so sentence | *It rained, so we stayed.* |
| **Cause (Conj)** | Because | Because + clause | *We stayed because it rained.* |
| **Cause (Prep)** | Because of | Because of + noun | *We stayed because of the rain.* |

---

## 4. Common Mistakes

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Correct |
|:---|:---|
| *Because of it was raining...* | *Because it was raining...* |
| *Due to he was sick...* | *Because he was sick...* / *Due to his illness...* |
| *It rained therefore we stayed.* | *It rained. Therefore, we stayed.* (punctuation!) |

---

## 5. Formality Scale

| Formal → Informal |
|:---|
| Hence > Therefore > Consequently > So |
| Owing to > Due to > Because of > Because |

---

## 6. Practice Exercises

### Choose the correct connector:
1. The roads were icy. ______, we drove slowly.
2. We cancelled the trip ______ bad weather.

> **Answers:**
> 1. Therefore/Consequently/Thus
> 2. because of / due to / owing to
