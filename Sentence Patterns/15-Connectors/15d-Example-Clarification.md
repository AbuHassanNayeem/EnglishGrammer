# Example & Clarification Connectors — Detailed Guide

## 1. Example Connectors

Introduce specific instances or illustrations of a general statement.

### For example / For instance
**Meaning:** Presenting a specific case

**Position:** Beginning of sentence (after comma) or mid-sentence (between commas)

**Examples:**
- *Many cities are polluted. **For example**, Beijing has severe smog.*
- *I enjoy outdoor activities, **for instance**, hiking and cycling.*

**Abbreviations:**
- **E.g.** (from Latin "exempli gratia") - used in formal writing
  - *I speak several languages (**e.g.**, French, Spanish, German).*

### Such as / Including
**Meaning:** Listing examples (not exhaustive)

**Structure:** Used **within** the sentence (not at the start)

**Examples:**
- *I enjoy sports **such as** tennis and swimming.*
- *Many countries, **including** Japan and Korea, have high-speed trains.*

**Note:** Don't use comma before "such as" when it's restrictive:
- ✅ *Sports such as tennis are expensive.*
- ❌ *Sports, such as tennis, are expensive.* (Unless you mean ALL sports)

### Namely (Complete List)
**Meaning:** Specifying ALL items (exhaustive list)

**Examples:**
- *Two students passed the test, **namely** John and Mary.* (ONLY these two)
- *I have one sibling, **namely** my sister.* (No others)

## 2. Clarification Connectors

Rephrase or explain the previous statement more clearly.

### That is / That is to say
**Meaning:** In other words / To put it differently

**Examples:**
- *He's a linguist, **that is**, he studies languages.*
- *The deadline is Friday, **that is to say**, you have three days.*

**Abbreviation:**
- **I.e.** (from Latin "id est")
  - *The deadline is Friday (**i.e.**, December 22nd).*

### In other words
**Meaning:** Restating for clarity

**Examples:**
- *The project failed. **In other words**, we wasted our time.*
- *He's allergic to dairy, **in other words**, he can't eat cheese.*

### To clarify / To put it another way
**Meaning:** Making the point clearer

**Examples:**
- ***To clarify**, the meeting is on Monday, not Tuesday.*
- ***To put it another way**, if you don't study, you won't pass.*

## 3. E.g. vs I.e.

| Abbreviation | Meaning | Usage | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **e.g.** | For example | Partial list | *I like fruits (e.g., apples, oranges).* |
| **i.e.** | That is | Clarification/complete | *My birthday (i.e., May 5th) is soon.* |

**Memory Trick:**
- **e.g.** = **e**xample **g**iven
- **i.e.** = **i**n **e**ssence

---

## 4. Summary Table

| Connector | Function | Position | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **For example** | Introduce example | Start/Mid | *For example, Paris is beautiful.* |
| **Such as** | List examples | Mid-sentence | *Cities such as Paris are beautiful.* |
| **Namely** | Complete list | Mid-sentence | *Two cities, namely Paris and Rome.* |
| **That is** | Clarify | Mid-sentence | *A linguist, that is, a language expert.* |
| **In other words** | Rephrase | Start/Mid | *In other words, he quit.* |

## 5. Practice Exercises

### Choose the correct connector:
1. I love outdoor activities, ______, hiking and camping.
2. The deadline is Friday, ______, December 22nd.

> **Answers:**
> 1. for example / for instance / such as
> 2. that is / i.e. / namely
