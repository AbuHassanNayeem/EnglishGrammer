# Sequence & Summary Connectors — Detailed Guide

## 1. Sequence/Order Connectors

Show the order of events, steps, or ideas.

### Firstly, Secondly, Thirdly (Formal)
**Usage:** Listing points in order

**Examples:**
- ***Firstly**, we need to analyze the data.**
- ***Secondly**, we must draw conclusions.**
- IMPORTANT*** Finally** (NOT "Fourthly" - uncommon), ***we will present results.*

### First, Second, Third (Less Formal)
**Examples:**
- ***First**, mix the ingredients.**
- ***Second**, heat the oven.**
- ***Third**, bake for 30 minutes.*

### Then / Next / After that
**Meaning:** What happens next

**Examples:**
- *We arrived at the hotel. **Then**, we checked in.*
- *Mix the flour. **Next**, add the eggs.*
- *I woke up. **After that**, I had breakfast.*

### Meanwhile / In the meantime
**Meaning:** At the same time / During this period

**Examples:**
- *I was cooking. **Meanwhile**, my sister was cleaning.*
- *The meeting is in an hour. **In the meantime**, let's prepare.*

### Subsequently / Afterwards (Formal)
**Meaning:** Later / After that point

**Examples:**
- *The company expanded. **Subsequently**, they hired more staff.*
- *We discussed the plan. **Afterwards**, we voted on it.*

### Finally / Lastly / In the end
**Meaning:** The last point / ultimate result

**Examples:**
- ***Finally**, I would like to thank everyone.*
- ***Lastly**, don't forget to submit your reports.*
- ***In the end**, we decided to cancel the project.*

## 2. Summary/Conclusion Connectors

Signal that you're wrapping up or summarizing.

### In conclusion (Academic)
**Usage:** Final paragraph of essays

**Example:**
- ***In conclusion**, this research demonstrates that...*

### To sum up / To summarize
**Usage:** Condensing what was said

**Examples:**
- ***To sum up**, we need more funding and time.*
- ***To summarize**, the project has three main phases.*

### In short / In brief (Condensing)
**Usage:** Quick summary

**Examples:**
- ***In short**, the plan failed.*
- ***In brief**, we cannot proceed without approval.*

### Overall / All in all
**Usage:** General assessment

**Examples:**
- ***Overall**, the event was a success.*
- ***All in all**, I'm satisfied with the results.*

## 3. Summary Table

| Type | Connector | Function | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **Sequence** | Firstly, Secondly | List order | *Firstly, we must plan.* |
| **Sequence** | Then, Next | Chronological | *First, mix. Then, heat.* |
| **Simultaneous** | Meanwhile | Same time | *I cooked. Meanwhile, she cleaned.* |
| **Later** | Subsequently | After that | *Subsequently, we voted.* |
| **Final** | Finally | Last point | *Finally, thank you all.* |
| **Summary** | In conclusion | Wrap up | *In conclusion, we agree.* |
| **Summary** | To sum up | Condense | *To sum up, it worked.* |

---

## 4. Common Mistakes

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Correct |
|:---|:---|
| *Fourthly, Fifthly...* | *Fourth, Fifth... **or** Finally* |
| *At the end, we decided...* | ***In the end**, we decided...* |
| *Firstable...* | ***Firstly** / **First*** |

---

## 5. Formality Scale

| Formal → Informal |
|:---|
| Subsequently > Then > After that |
| In conclusion > To sum up > In short |
| Firstly > First |

## 6. Practice Exercises

### Choose the correct connector:
1. ______, let me thank everyone.

> **Answers:**
> 1. Finally / Lastly
> 2. Subsequently / Then / Afterwards
