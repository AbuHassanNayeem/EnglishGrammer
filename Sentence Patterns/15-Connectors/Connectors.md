## 1. Introduction
**Connectors** (also called linking words, discourse markers, or transition words) are words or phrases that link sentences and paragraphs, creating coherent and logical flow in writing and speech.

This chapter covers all major connector categories.

## 4. Punctuation Rules

### Conjunctive Adverbs (However, Therefore)
- Sentence**. Connector,** sentence.
- Sentence**; connector,** sentence.

**Example:** *It rained. However, we went out.*

### Coordinating Conjunctions (And, But, So)
- Sentence**, connector** sentence.

**Example:** *It rained, but we went out.*

### Subordinating Conjunctions (Although, Because)
- **Connector** clause, main clause.
- Main clause **connector** clause.

**Example:** *Although it rained, we went out.*
