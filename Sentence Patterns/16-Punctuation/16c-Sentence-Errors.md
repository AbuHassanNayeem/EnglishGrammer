# Sentence Errors — Detailed Guide

## 1. Comma Splice

### Definition
Joining two independent clauses with **only** a comma (incorrect).

### Examples of Error
❌ *I went to the store, I bought milk.*
❌ *She is smart, she studies hard.*

### How to Fix

**Option 1: Period**
- ✅ *I went to the store**.** I bought milk.*

**Option 2: Semicolon**
- ✅ *I went to the store**;** I bought milk.*

**Option 3: Coordinating Conjunction (FANBOYS)**
- ✅ *I went to the store**, and** I bought milk.*

**Option 4: Subordination**
- ✅ *I went to the store **because** I needed milk.*
- ✅ ***When** I went to the store, I bought milk.*

---

## 2. Run-On Sentence (Fused Sentence)

### Definition
Joining two independent clauses with **no** punctuation (incorrect).

### Examples of Error
❌ *I went to the store I bought milk.*
❌ *She is smart she studies hard.*

### How to Fix
**Same fixes as comma splice:**
1. Period
2. Semicolon
3. Conjunction with comma
4. Subordination

---

## 3. Sentence Fragment

### Definition
An **incomplete** sentence punctuated as if it were complete.

### Common Types

**A. Dependent Clause Alone**
❌ *Because I was tired.* (NOT a complete sentence)
✅ ***Because I was tired**, I went to bed.*
✅ *I went to bed **because I was tired**.*

**B. Missing Subject**
❌ *Went to the store.* (Who went?)
✅ ***I** went to the store.*

**C. Missing Verb**
❌ *The tall boy in the blue shirt.* (What about him?)
✅ *The tall boy in the blue shirt **is my brother**.*

**D. Phrase Alone**
❌ *Running quickly through the park.* (Who? What happened?)
✅ ***Running quickly through the park**, I saw a deer.*

### Intentional Fragments (Acceptable)
In informal writing or speech, fragments can be stylistic:
- *"Where are you going?" "To the store."* (Acceptable reply)
- *Beautiful day, isn't it?* (Conversational)

---

## 4. Summary: How to Identify

| Error | Test | Fix |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Comma Splice** | Two independent clauses + only comma | Add conjunction, use semicolon, or split |
| **Run-On** | Two independent clauses + no punctuation | Add punctuation or conjunction |
| **Fragment** | Cannot stand alone as sentence | Add subject, verb, or attach to main clause |

### Independence Test
Can it stand alone and make complete sense?
- ✅ *I went home.* (Yes = Complete)
- ❌ *Because I was tired.* (No = Fragment)

## 5. Practice Exercises

### Identify and fix the error:
1. I love reading, my sister prefers movies.
2. Running through the park late at night.
3. She studied hard therefore she passed.

> **Answers:**
> 1. Comma splice. Fix: *I love reading**; however,** my sister prefers movies.*
> 2. Fragment. Fix: ***While** running through the park late at night, I saw a fox.*
> 3. Run-on. Fix: *She studied hard**; therefore,** she passed.*
