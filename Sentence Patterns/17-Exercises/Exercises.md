# Exercises — Practice Problems

This section contains practice exercises for the key sentence patterns and grammatical elements. Work through each section and check your answers at the end.

## Exercise 1: Identify Sentence Components (Subject, Verb, Object, Complement)

Label each underlined element using `S` (subject), `V` (verb), `O` (object), `C` (complement), or `A` (adverbial).

1. **She** reads books. → S
2. The sky **looks** blue. → V
3. They elected **him president**. → O + C
4. He sleeps **early**. → A
5. **I** sent her a message. → S
6. The movie **made** me sad. → V
7. She **gave** me a pen. → V
8. Birds **fly**. → V
9. The book **is** interesting. → V
10. He lives **in Dhaka**. → A

## Exercise 3: Correct Subject-Verb Agreement

Choose the correct verb form:

1. The team **is / are** winning.
   - Answer: **is** (singular; team viewed as one unit)
2. Either he or his friends **is / are** coming.
   - Answer: **are** (plural; "friends" is nearer)
3. One of the students **was / were** absent.
   - Answer: **was** (singular; "one" is subject)
4. There **is / are** several cats on the roof.
   - Answer: **are** (plural; "cats" is the real subject)
5. All of the cake **has / have** been eaten.
   - Answer: **has** (singular; "cake" is singular)
6. The list of items **is / are** on the table.
   - Answer: **is** (singular; "list" is subject)
7. Neither the teachers nor the principal **was / were** informed.
   - Answer: **was** (singular; "principal" is nearer)
8. News **is / are** on the radio.
   - Answer: **is** (singular; "news" always singular despite -s)
9. Both students **has / have** finished.
   - Answer: **have** (plural; "both" is plural)
10. Everyone **wants / want** to leave.
    - Answer: **wants** (singular; "everyone" is singular)

## Exercise 4: Identify Object Types

Label each underlined object as **DO** (direct object) or **IO** (indirect object):

1. She gave **him** a pen. → IO
2. I sent **the letter** yesterday. → DO
3. They asked **her** a question. → IO (and "question" is DO)
4. He showed **me** his passport. → IO
5. We elected **him president**. → DO (+ complement)

## Exercise 5: Convert Active to Passive

Rewrite each sentence in the passive voice:

1. She reads the book.
   - Answer: The book is read by her.
2. They elected him president.
   - Answer: He was elected president.
3. I sent her a message.
   - Answer: A message was sent to her (by me). / She was sent a message (by me).
4. The teacher gave the students an assignment.
   - Answer: An assignment was given to the students by the teacher. / The students were given an assignment by the teacher.
5. They found the solution.
   - Answer: The solution was found by them.

## Exercise 6: Identify and Correct Inversion

Rewrite inverted sentences in normal order:

1. Here comes the bus.
   - Answer: The bus comes here.
2. Never have I seen such beauty.
   - Answer: I have never seen such beauty.
3. Down fell the leaves.
   - Answer: The leaves fell down.
4. Are you ready?
   - Answer: You are ready.

## Exercise 7: Noun, Adjective, and Adverbial Clauses

Identify the type of clause (NC = noun clause, AC = adjective clause, AdvC = adverbial clause):

1. The student who worked hard passed. → AC
2. I know that she left. → NC
3. When the bell rang, everyone left. → AdvC
4. The book that I read was excellent. → AC
5. She will come if she has time. → AdvC
6. What he said surprised me. → NC
7. The person who helped us was kind. → AC
8. Although it rained, we went outside. → AdvC

## Exercise 8: Transitive and Separable Phrasal Verbs

Rewrite using the alternative object placement (if applicable):

1. She looked up the word.
   - Answer: She looked the word up.
2. I ran into an old friend.
   - Answer: (inseparable — no alternative)
3. Pick up the trash.
   - Answer: Pick the trash up.
4. He turned on the light.
   - Answer: He turned the light on.

Label each underlined complement as **SC** (subject complement) or **OC** (object complement):

## Exercise 10: Spot the Error

Identify and correct the grammatical error in each sentence:

1. **Her and me went to the store.**
   - Error: Subject pronouns used as objects.
   - Correction: She and I went to the store.

2. **The list of items are on the table.**
   - Error: Subject-verb disagreement.
   - Correction: The list of items is on the table.

3. **He explained the concept without.** (incomplete)
   - Error: Missing required object.
   - Correction: He explained the concept.

4. **The team are winning today.** (British interpretation might differ)
   - Error: Team viewed as singular unit.
   - Correction (American): The team is winning today.

5. **She looked up it.**
   - Error: Separable phrasal verb with pronoun in wrong position.
   - Correction: She looked it up.

|----------|-------|------------|
| 1 | Components | Identify S, V, O, C, A |
| 2 | Transitivity | Transitive verbs take objects; intransitive do not |
| 3 | Agreement | Verb must match subject in number; watch for "and", "either...or", collective nouns |
| 4 | Object Types | DO answers "what/whom"; IO answers "to/for whom" |
| 5 | Passive | Object becomes subject; use "by" for agent |
| 6 | Inversion | Reverse verb and subject for normal order in questions/some stylistic sentences |
| 7 | Clauses | NC functions as noun; AC modifies noun; AdvC modifies verb |
| 8 | Phrasal Verbs | Separable verbs allow object between parts; inseparable do not |
| 9 | Complements | SC describes subject; OC describes object |
| 10 | Error Spotting | Watch for case, agreement, fragments, word order |
